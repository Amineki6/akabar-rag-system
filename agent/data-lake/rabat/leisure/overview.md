### Overview of Rabat's Attractions

The provided documents describe Rabat's attractions as a blend of significant historical landmarks and a modern, sophisticated urban experience. Key historical sites include the picturesque, blue-and-white Kasbah of the Udayas, the iconic Hassan Tower with the adjacent Mausoleum of Mohammed V, and the atmospheric Chellah necropolis, which contains both Roman and Islamic ruins. The city also hosts major cultural institutions such as the Museum Mohamed VI of Modern and Contemporary Art, the National Archaeology Museum, and the Oudaia Museum, which is dedicated to Moroccan jewelry and is located within the Kasbah.

Popular neighborhoods for tourists include the historic Medina, which is ideal for a culturally immersive stay in a traditional riad; the modern Ville Nouvelle (Downtown) with its many hotels and restaurants; and the trendy, upscale district of Agdal. Shopping experiences range from the traditional souks in the Medina, particularly on the famous Rue des Consuls, to the modern boutiques and international brands found in the Agdal neighborhood and the large Mega Mall.

For leisure and entertainment, visitors can relax in tranquil gardens like the Andalusian Gardens within the Kasbah or visit the modern Jardin Zoologique de Rabat. The city's modern side can be explored by riding the efficient tramway that connects Rabat to Salé. The nightlife is generally relaxed and sophisticated, with a concentration of trendy bars and lounges in the Agdal neighborhood and unique venues like Le Dhow, a restaurant on a boat moored on the river. Rabat is also a major cultural hub, hosting large-scale events like the Mawazine music festival.
