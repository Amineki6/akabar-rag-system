### Where to Eat

#### The Medina
The historic heart of Rabat is filled with traditional restaurants and small, family-run eateries tucked away in its winding alleys. Dining in the Medina offers an authentic experience, with a focus on classic Moroccan home cooking. It's the best place to find a delicious, traditional tagine or a hearty bowl of Harira.

#### Ville Nouvelle (Downtown)
The modern city center, built during the French Protectorate, is home to a wide range of dining options. Here you'll find classic French-style brasseries, established Moroccan restaurants, and numerous cafes perfect for a coffee and a pastry.

#### Agdal
This vibrant and upscale neighborhood is known for its modern and international dining scene. The streets of Agdal are lined with trendy cafes, fast-food chains, and restaurants serving cuisine from around the world, including Italian, Asian, and French. It's a popular spot for a more contemporary dining experience.

#### The Marina and Riverfront
The area around the Bou Regreg river marina, which separates Rabat from its sister city Salé, offers a more upscale dining experience with stunning views. The waterfront is lined with modern cafes and restaurants perfect for enjoying a meal while watching the boats and seeing the Hassan Tower and Kasbah in the distance.
