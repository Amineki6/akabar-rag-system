### The French Protectorate and Modern Capital

Rabat's modern history began in 1912 with the establishment of the **French Protectorate**. The French administrator, **Hubert Lyautey**, made the momentous decision to designate Rabat as the administrative capital of the protectorate. He felt its coastal location was more practical than the interior cities of Fes or Marrakech.

Lyautey commissioned the French architect and urban planner **Henri Prost** to design a modern administrative quarter, the **Ville Nouvelle**, adjacent to the old medina. Prost created a new city with wide, tree-lined avenues, elegant ministerial buildings, and public gardens, carefully preserving Rabat's historic landmarks. This period defined the layout and architectural character of modern Rabat.
