### The Republic of Salé

For centuries, Rabat was overshadowed by its neighbor, Salé. The city's fortunes were revived in the early 17th century with the arrival of thousands of **Moriscos** (Muslims expelled from Spain). These new inhabitants, possessing advanced skills in seafaring and warfare, settled in the old Kasbah and established an independent city-state known as the **Republic of Salé**.

For several decades, this republic became a notorious and powerful base for the **Barbary Pirates** (also known as the Salé Rovers). These pirates raided ships as far away as the British Isles and Iceland, capturing goods and enslaving crews, making the republic a significant maritime power.

---

### Alaouite Rule and Imperial Status

The pirate republic was eventually brought under the control of the central Moroccan government by the **Alaouite dynasty** in the late 17th century. The Alaouites unified Rabat and Salé and recognized Rabat's importance. Although sultans moved the capital between cities like Fes and Meknes, Rabat was often used as an imperial residence.
