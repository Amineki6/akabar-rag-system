Under the reign of **King Mohammed VI**, Essaouira has experienced a remarkable revival, transforming from a city once marginalized under the French Protectorate into a major cultural and tourist destination. The king's personal involvement has been a key factor in this transformation.

### Urban Development and Rehabilitation

King Mohammed VI has initiated extensive rehabilitation programs for Essaouira's medina, a designated **UNESCO World Heritage site**. These projects aim to preserve the city's historical architectural character, restore its heritage, and improve the living conditions of its residents. The goal is to enhance Essaouira's appeal as a tourist and economic hub while respecting its unique identity.

### Preservation of Jewish-Moroccan Heritage

Essaouira has long been a symbol of coexistence and tolerance between different religious communities. King Mohammed VI has highlighted this heritage, emphasizing the legitimate place of Jewish people in Morocco's history. A significant event was his 2020 visit to **Bayt Dakira** ("The House of Memory"), a unique spiritual and historical space dedicated to preserving and celebrating the Jewish-Moroccan memory. This site, which includes the "Slat Attia" synagogue, stands as a testament to the royal will to protect and celebrate the kingdom's cultural and spiritual diversity.

### Essaouira as a City of Arts and Culture

King Mohammed VI has also supported Essaouira's development as a cultural center. The city hosts numerous prominent cultural events, and the monarch's attention has helped reinforce its image as the "City of the Alizés" (trade winds). Major projects, such as the launch of the "City of Arts and Culture" in 2020, illustrate this commitment to making Essaouira a hub for artistic creation and exchange.

In short, the era of Mohammed VI has been a pivotal period for Essaouira. The king's dedication has helped to re-launch the city, not as a commercial port, but as a premier destination where history, culture, and tolerance are at the heart of its modern-day revival.
