Chefchaouen is a compact city, and its main leisure areas are not so much distinct neighborhoods as they are the various sections of its main historical area, the medina, and the surrounding natural spaces. The city is often divided into two main parts: the old city (medina) and the new city (ville nouvelle). For tourists, almost all activities and attractions are concentrated within the enchanting medina.

Here's a breakdown of the key areas and what they offer:

### The Old Medina
This is the heart and soul of Chefchaouen, the famous "Blue Pearl" that attracts visitors from around the world. The medina is the most popular and central area for all leisure activities.

* **Plaza Uta el-Hammam:** This is the main square and the vibrant hub of the medina. It's surrounded by the city's most important landmarks, including the Kasbah and the Grand Mosque. The plaza is filled with cafes and restaurants with terraces, making it the perfect spot for people-watching, enjoying a meal, or sipping mint tea.
* **The Kasbah:** Situated right on Plaza Uta el-Hammam, this historic fortress is a key part of the medina's leisure offerings. It houses a museum, a beautiful Andalusian garden, and its tower offers spectacular views of the blue city.
* **Souika:** This is one of the oldest neighborhoods within the medina, dating back to the 15th century. Its name means "Little Souk" and it was historically a market area. Today, it remains a bustling section with shops and vendors.
* **Ras El Maa:** Located just outside the medina's northeastern gate, this area is a popular gathering place where the city's main water source flows. You can find local women doing laundry and it’s a tranquil spot to relax by the water.

### The New City (Ville Nouvelle)
While less of a tourist attraction, this area has its own charms and is where you'll find the local market on Mondays and Thursdays. It's a great place to see daily Moroccan life beyond the tourist bubble.

### Beyond the City
Chefchaouen's leisure experience extends to the stunning natural landscape that surrounds it.

* **Talassemtane National Park:** For those who enjoy hiking and nature, this national park is a popular destination. The main attractions here are the scenic trails leading to the **Akchour Waterfalls** and the impressive natural rock formation known as **God's Bridge**.
* **The Spanish Mosque:** A short hike up the hill outside the medina leads you to this abandoned mosque. It's not a neighborhood but a very popular leisure spot, especially at sunset, as it provides the most iconic panoramic view of the entire blue medina.
