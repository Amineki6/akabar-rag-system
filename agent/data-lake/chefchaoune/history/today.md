Chefchaouen today is a vibrant blend of its rich history and a modern identity shaped primarily by tourism. It has successfully maintained its unique character while embracing its status as a top global travel destination.

### Economy and Tourism

Tourism is the lifeblood of Chefchaouen's modern economy. The city has become a "must-see" destination, particularly for its aesthetic appeal, and this has led to a boom in small businesses.

* **Hospitality:** The medina is filled with guesthouses (riads and dars), small hotels, and restaurants catering to international visitors.
* **Handicrafts:** Local artisans thrive by selling their unique products, including woolen clothing, leather goods, and handwoven baskets, in the city's souks.
* **Eco-Tourism:** Leveraging its location in the Rif Mountains, the city is also focusing on eco-tourism and sustainable development, with hiking and nature excursions being a major draw.

### Culture and Society

Despite the significant tourist traffic, Chefchaouen has largely retained its relaxed, traditional atmosphere.

* **Preservation of Heritage:** The local community is highly committed to preserving its cultural identity. Traditional practices, such as baking bread in communal ovens and engaging in collective community spaces, remain an important part of daily life.
* **Multilingualism:** Due to its history, Spanish is widely spoken, particularly by the older generation, alongside Moroccan Arabic. French and English are also common, especially among those working in tourism.
* **Art and Creativity:** The city has become a hub for artists and photographers, inspired by its distinctive blue aesthetic. Numerous art galleries showcase the work of local and international artists.

### Challenges and Future

As with any major tourist destination, Chefchaouen faces challenges related to sustainability and the impact of tourism on local authenticity. In recent years, the city has been actively working on a strategic vision for its future, focusing on sustainable urban development and preserving its unique charm. This includes initiatives to improve public services and promote responsible tourism, ensuring that Chefchaouen remains a cherished and authentic "blue pearl" for generations to come.
