### Where to Eat

#### The Medina & Kasbah
The winding streets of the old city are filled with a variety of dining options. You can find small, authentic eateries serving traditional dishes like Bissara and tagine, as well as more upscale riad restaurants offering a refined dining experience. The Kasbah has several restaurants with rooftop terraces that provide stunning views of the port and the sea.

#### The Port and Beachfront
For the freshest seafood, head to the restaurants located near the port and along the beachfront promenade (Avenue Mohammed VI). These establishments serve the day's catch, from grilled fish to seafood paella, often with a view of the water.

#### The Ville Nouvelle (New City)
The modern part of Tangier offers a wide range of international cuisine, from Spanish tapas bars to French bistros and Italian restaurants. The area around Boulevard Pasteur is a hub for cafes and restaurants with a more European feel.

#### Historic Cafes
Tangier is famous for its legendary cafes, which were once the haunts of writers, artists. Visiting places like **Café de Paris**, **Café Hafa**, or **Gran Cafe de la Poste** for a coffee or mint tea is an essential Tangier experience, offering a glimpse into the city's storied past.
