### Overview of Tangier's Attractions

Based on the provided files, Tangier's attractions offer a captivating mix of history, diverse cultures, and stunning coastal scenery. The city's historic heart is the **Medina** and the **Kasbah**, the ancient fortress perched at its highest point. Key landmarks include the bustling squares of **Petit Socco** and **Grand Socco**, the unique **St. Andrew's Church** with its blend of English and Moroccan styles, and the famous viewpoint **Terrasse des Paresseux**. The city also hosts several significant museums, such as the **Kasbah Museum of Mediterranean Cultures**, the **Ibn Battouta Museum**, and the **Tangier American Legation Museum**, the only U.S. National Historic Landmark in a foreign country.

***

Popular neighborhoods for tourists include the historic **Medina** and **Kasbah** for an immersive cultural stay, the modern **Ville Nouvelle** with its European architecture and amenities, and the **Beachfront** area for a resort-like experience. Shopping ranges from the traditional souks of the Medina, where bargaining is expected, to the modern, fixed-price boutiques on **Boulevard Pasteur** in the Ville Nouvelle and large shopping centers like **Tanger City Mall**.

***

For leisure and entertainment, major attractions lie along the coast, including the mythological **Caves of Hercules** and **Cap Spartel**, the northwesternmost point of Africa where the Atlantic and Mediterranean meet. Visitors can relax in lush green spaces like **Parc Perdicaris**. The nightlife is vibrant and varied, featuring chic **rooftop bars** in the Kasbah with views of the strait, modern clubs in the Ville Nouvelle, and the quintessential Tangier experience of sipping mint tea at a **historic cafe** like the cliffside **Café Hafa**. The city is also known for its annual **Tanjazz** music festival.
