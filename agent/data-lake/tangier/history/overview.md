### Overview of Tangier's History

The provided documents trace the history of Tangier from its ancient origins as a Phoenician trading post established as early as the 10th century BC. It later became the capital of the Roman province of Mauretania Tingitana under the name Tingis. In the early 8th century, the city was conquered by Arab Umayyad forces and served as a crucial base for the invasion of the Iberian Peninsula in 711.

Its strategic location made it a target for European powers; it was captured by the Portuguese in 1471 and was later passed to England in 1661 as part of a royal dowry. The English abandoned the city in 1684, and it was reclaimed by Moroccan forces under Sultan Moulay Ismail.

In the 20th century, Tangier gained a unique status when it was designated an International Zone in 1923, governed by a commission of foreign powers. This period gave the city a legendary reputation as a hub for espionage and a haven for expatriate writers like Paul Bowles and William S. Burroughs.

Tangier was officially reunified with Morocco upon the country's independence in 1956. After a period of neglect, the city has undergone a dramatic renaissance since the 21st century, with massive investment in projects like the Tanger Med port, transforming it into a vibrant economic hub.
