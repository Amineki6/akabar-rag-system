Historical Sites and Viewpoints In Taroudant:
Ramparts surrounding the center of Taroudant
Surrounded by its impressive earth-colored ramparts, Taroudant is a city that has managed to preserve its authenticity. Formerly the capital of the Saadians in the 16th century before they moved their throne to Marrakech, it contains remarkable architectural and cultural heritage that bears witness to its rich history.The five gates of the city
Bab El Kasbah, which leads to the Pasha's kasbah, with its vaulted passages and typical geometric patterns.
Bab Ouled Ali, Historical, beautiful decorative patterns, imposing facade, entry point to the souk.
Bab El Hed, Small gate, but rich in architectural details, historically used for trade.
Bab al-Zarkan – Named after "Izrkan," which means mills set on waters to grind grains and sugar cane since antiquity. It is the gate located near the bus station of Taroudant.
Bab Targunt: It was formerly called Bab al-Ghaz, which was the gate through which the army was sent on its military campaigns. It has been redesigned and features a staircase next to it, similar to that of Bab al-Silsila, and through this staircase, you can ascend to the Targunt gate, take souvenir photos, and enjoy a panoramic view of Mohammed V Street, which leads into the Targunt gate neighborhood
.Bab Awlad Bouna: The name of this gate is attributed to Andalusian families who settled in Taroudant. Work is currently underway to rehabilitate Bab Ouled Bennouna to transform it into a museum.

Collective granaries “agadir” The region around Taliouine is a treasure of archaeological and historical sites, many of which take the form of ancient wells and Agadir. Agadir refers to a type of castle or fortified structures that were commonly built in this region in the past.

Agadir Tasguent 
is an amazing illustration of traditional architecture and the representation and preservation of historical resilience in Morocco, just two hours' drive from Taroudant.

Ifri Imadidene’s agadir
 is one of the rare collective granaries under rock and partly troglodyte still in operation. Located some 17 km from Taliouine on the way to Ouarzazate, the granary of Ifri Imadidene is an architectural masterpiece of the region.
The granary is located under the water source which comes from the plateau. It is separated from the village by fields of cereals and almond trees.

In Taliouine:
The saffron harvest takes place at the end of October or the beginning of November, and usually occurs at sunrise when the sun's rays are still pleasant. During this critical period, the harvesters carefully select each crimson stigma or pistil at the center of the saffron flowers before the heat of the day arrives.

The summit of the ancient volcanic mount SirouineAt 3304 meters in altitude, this impressive mountain is widely regarded as one of the best spots in Taliouine and among the top hiking destinations in Morocco. It offers the perfect opportunity for hiking and immersing oneself in the natural beauty of the Little Atlas region.

Here are some tips to make the most of your visit:
Visit the Kasbah of Tiout: This historical fortress, dating back to the 16th century, offers stunning views of the palm grove and surrounding villages.
Explore the palm grove: With over 20,000 palm trees, the palm grove of Tiout is a true haven of peace and freshness.
Discover local souks and markets: Enjoy the traditional markets vibrantly full of energy and local specialties.
Take part in excursions: Board a camel caravan to explore the dunes of Merzouga or the ksar of Ouarzazate.
Hiking and outdoor activities: The Atlas Mountains and the gorges of the Grand South offer unforgettable trekking experiences
