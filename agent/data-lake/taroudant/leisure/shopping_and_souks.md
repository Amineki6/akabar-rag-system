A guide to the souks and markets in Taroudant

Taroudant has three souks 
* ** ("markets" in Arabic), each with its own characteristics. 
* ** The Amazigh souk, located at Derb Jdid, offers a multitude of shops and boutiques, ranging from small craft stores to fruits and vegetables, clothing, kitchen utensils, and more. This souk is primarily intended for daily shopping for the local population. It is quite authentic and picturesque, and the merchants there offer a warm welcome, far from the harassment found in the souk of Marrakech.

* ** The artisan souk two hundred meters from the Riad Taroudant is more specialized in craftsmanship, particularly that of leather and silver. You will find a multitude of shops that will make you happy. If you want to shop, don't hesitate to negotiate hard. This will allow you to save money, but, above all, to play the game, take the time to chat and drink tea with the merchant who will be delighted to meet you.
The jewelry souk of Taroudant It is located near the Jnan Ejjamaa municipal market, benefiting from a more serene and relaxed atmosphere. Visiting the stalls gives you the opportunity to discover the subtlety of traditional Berber jewelry designs. Like the markets of the Middle Ages, the traditional organization of the Souks is done by trade. Even today, the traditional jewelers of the city occupy a small area at the end of a maze of alleys accessed from Talmoklate square.

* ** The Sunday souk, as its name indicates, only takes place on Sundays, outside the city walls heading towards Tamaloukt. It is a vast market where buyers and sellers come from the surrounding mountains and countryside. It is very rustic, very lively, full of all sorts of goods for sale, but mainly essential products. The ideal way to get there is by horse-drawn carriage, which makes for a lovely ride around the ramparts.


Atmosphere and TipsThe souk exudes a wonderful atmosphere of a traditional Amazigh market, with vendors calling out to attract customers and typical bustling activity.Always ask for permission before taking photos of people.Prefer cash payments, as the market is essentially cash only. Plan to have small bills or coins for purchases.Be prepared to negotiate politely to get the best price, especially on handmade items.

* ** The Babouches "Les balghas" and the Craftsmanship of TaroudantThe Taroudant babouche is designed by experienced Moroccan artisans. It is made from high-quality leathers tanned using vegetable methods. This babouche is a must-have for lovers of comfortable and elegant shoes. You can observe some artisans at work and purchase unique authentic pieces. Local shops also offer carpets, handicrafts, and handmade textiles from the region of the Anti-Atlas and Grand Atlas mountains.

The craftsmanship of Taroudant is a renowned activity, specializing in leather making, wrought iron, wood carving, jewelry, carpets, as well as metalwork.

Other Nearby Markets and Souks:

* ** The souks around Taroudant will immerse you and plunge you into the authenticity of the region's rural populations. People come here to stock up and socialize. A particular feature of the region: you will find the Wholesale Market – Souk Jamla located in Oulad Teima.

* ** SOUK EL TNINE D’ISSEN
The rural municipality of Issen (30.434595, -9.251973), halfway between Agadir and Taroudant, has its weekly souk every Monday. This is a day with particular excitement anticipated by the local population. Taking advantage of its proximity to the hinterland of Taroudant and Agadir, this souk is very well stocked with fresh produce, vegetables, citrus fruits, and other goods. A typical rural souk of the region.

* ** SOUK DE TIOUTE
Located 30 km south of Taroudant and about 100 km east of Agadir, the village of Tioute (30.394840, -8.687970) is a small village on the road to Tata, which features a lush palm grove worth visiting. Every Wednesday, you will find in this village the weekly souk, which has managed to preserve all its authenticity.

* ** Souk de Taliouine
Located about 100km north east of Taroudant, this lively weekly market is organized every Monday morning and serves as the heart and soul of the local community, showcasing and selling crafts, products, and specialties from the surrounding area.

* ** Practical Information

The souk generally takes place early in the morning until mid or late afternoon. The market is very busy around 10-11 am, which is the ideal time to observe local life and enjoy the atmosphere. Nearby, you will find cafes and small restaurants where you can eat and taste local specialties such as tajine or Moroccan pastries.

In conclusion, visiting the weekly souks of Taroudant is an unmissable experience for those who want to discover Amazigh culture, local flavors, and the unique craftsmanship of this region of the Sous.
