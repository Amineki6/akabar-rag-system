Taroudant, often called "Little Marrakech," offers a more relaxed and authentic Moroccan experience. The city's leisure activities are centered around its historical medina, vibrant souks, and beautiful natural surroundings.

### Exploring the City

* **City Walls (Ramparts):** The city is encircled by ancient, ochre-colored ramparts that are approximately 7.5 kilometers long. A popular activity is to walk, bike, or take a horse-drawn carriage ride along these walls. At certain points, you can climb the ramparts for panoramic views of the city and the surrounding Anti-Atlas Mountains, especially at sunset.
* **Souks (Markets):** Taroudant is known for its two main souks: the Arab Souk and the Berber Souk. These markets are a great place to experience daily Moroccan life and shop for traditional handicrafts. You'll find a wide variety of goods, including leatherwork, carpets, silver Berber jewelry, spices, and local argan oil.
* **Assarag Square and Place 20 Ghoucht:** These are the two main plazas in the medina, offering a great starting point for exploring. They are lively hubs where you can sit at a cafe, sip mint tea, and watch the world go by.
* **Traditional Hammam:** For a relaxing and cultural experience, visit a local hammam (public bathhouse). This is a traditional way to unwind and cleanse.

### Day Trips and Outdoor Activities

* **Tiout Oasis:** Located about 30 kilometers from Taroudant, the Tiout Oasis is a stunning palm grove at the foot of an old kasbah. You can explore the lush greenery on foot or by a donkey ride, and enjoy a traditional lunch with a view of the oasis from the kasbah.
* **Hiking and Trekking:** Taroudant is a gateway to the High Atlas and Anti-Atlas Mountains. You can arrange guided hiking or trekking tours for one or more days to explore the rugged landscapes, traditional Berber villages, and beautiful valleys.
* **Horseback Riding and Quad Biking:** For a more adventurous outing, you can go horseback riding or quad biking to explore the landscapes around the city, including argan tree forests and dry riverbeds (wadis).
* **Claudio Bravo Palace:** Visit the stunning palace and gardens of the late Chilean painter, Claudio Bravo, which showcase a beautiful collection of art and exotic plants.

### Culture and History

* **Tanneries:** Just outside the city walls, you can visit the local tanneries to see the traditional process of leather making. The leather goods in the souks are often sourced from here.
* **Architecture:** The city's historical sites include the Grand Mosque and the Arrahma Mosque, which offer a glimpse into the city's past and Islamic architecture.
* **Local Cuisine:** Taroudant offers a chance to enjoy authentic Moroccan food. Be sure to try local pastries and traditional dishes like tagine.
