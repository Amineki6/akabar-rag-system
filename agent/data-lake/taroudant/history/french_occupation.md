The history of Taroudant during the French occupation is a reflection of the resistance that marked southern Morocco. Unlike other regions of the country that were "pacified" relatively early, the Souss region and the area around Taroudant experienced fierce and prolonged resistance.

### General Context of the French Occupation in Morocco

In 1912, France established its protectorate over Morocco with the signing of the Treaty of Fez. However, this treaty did not end the resistance. In many regions, especially in rural and mountainous areas, local tribes continued to fiercely oppose the French presence.

### Resistance in Taroudant and the Souss

* **A late defeat of the resistance:** Taroudant and the Souss region were strongholds of resistance against the colonizer. The French only established effective control over the city in 1917, several years after the beginning of the protectorate.
* **The role of Ahmed al-Hiba:** The resistance in southern Morocco was led by figures like Ahmed al-Hiba, a tribal and religious leader. In 1912, he managed to subdue a large part of the Souss region and take control of Agadir and Taroudant. He even marched on Marrakech but was defeated by French forces at the Battle of Sidi Bou Othman in 1912. He withdrew to the south and continued the resistance until his death in 1919.
* **Control and administration:** Once control was established, the protectorate authorities set up a local administration. For example, a pacha (governor) was appointed, often chosen from influential local families known for their loyalty to France.
* **The impact of the occupation:** Under the protectorate, the role of Taroudant changed somewhat. While it had been a caravan city and a historical capital of the Saadian dynasty, its economic importance declined in favor of coastal cities like Agadir. However, it retained its character as a traditional city of the Souss.

The occupation of Taroudant and the Souss region illustrates the complexity and violence of the French "pacification," which lasted for several decades and faced determined resistance from the local population.
