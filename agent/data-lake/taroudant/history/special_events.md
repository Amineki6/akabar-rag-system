Taroudant, with its long and rich history, has been a key location for several significant events and continues to be a hub for cultural celebrations. While its most defining historical moments are tied to its political and military past, the city today is a vibrant center for art and tradition.

### Historical Events

* **The Saadian Golden Age:** The most significant period in Taroudant's history was the 16th century when it served as the capital of the Saadian dynasty under Sultan Mohammed al-Sheikh. This was a time of great prosperity, as the city became a major center for the trade of gold, sugar, and salt, and its iconic ramparts were built during this era to fortify it.
* **A Center of Resistance:** Taroudant played a crucial role in the early 20th century resistance against the French protectorate. The city served as a key base for the resistance leader Ahmed al-Hiba, who used it as a capital to oppose the French forces. The city's inhabitants and its surrounding tribes put up a fierce fight, and it was one of the last areas in Morocco to fall under French control, not being "pacified" until 1917.

### Modern Special Events and Festivals

Today, Taroudant's special events are focused on celebrating its rich cultural heritage, particularly its Amazigh (Berber) and musical traditions.

* **Festival National de l'Art Griha et Malhoun:** This national festival is dedicated to the art of Griha and Malhoun, which are traditional forms of Moroccan poetry and music. The event, which takes place in the city, aims to preserve and promote this cultural heritage, with performances, tributes to artists, and exhibitions.
* **Izourane Traditional Arts Festival:** The province of Taroudant hosts this festival, which celebrates traditional Amazigh arts, with a particular focus on the collective dance known as *Ahwach*. The event brings together artistic troupes from different regions and includes dance performances, music concerts, and workshops. It is a key event for preserving and showcasing Amazigh culture.
* **Taroudant Horse Festival:** As an important agricultural and horse-rearing region, Taroudant also hosts a horse festival. This event highlights the deep-rooted connection between Moroccans and their steeds, often featuring *Fantasia* performances, a traditional exhibition of horsemanship.
