# Overview of Moroccan Culture: Literature

Moroccan literature is a multilingual tapestry written in Arabic, amazigh, Hebrew, and French. Historically linked to the great literary traditions of Al-Andalus, it experienced a modern rebirth in the 20th century.

### Historical Highlights
* The University of al-Qarawiyyin in Fes was a major medieval literary and scholarly hub, attracting figures like Maimonides and Ibn Khaldun.
* The Almohad dynasty fostered a brilliant period of learning, with the Kutubiyya Mosque in Marrakesh becoming famous for its libraries and book shops.

### The Modern Era
* Modern Moroccan literature began in the 1930s, shaped by contact with European and other Arab literary movements.
* Key 20th-century authors include Mohamed Choukri, Driss Chraïbi, and Mohamed Zafzaf, as well as francophone writers like Tahar Ben Jelloun,Laila Lalami,mohamed berada,Mbarek Rabi;Leïla Slimani;Driss Chraïbi;Mohammed Khaïr-Eddine
* In the 1950s and 1960s, Morocco became an artistic refuge for international writers like Paul Bowles and William S. Burroughs.
* Oral literature (orature), in both Moroccan Arabic and Berber, remains an integral part of the culture.
