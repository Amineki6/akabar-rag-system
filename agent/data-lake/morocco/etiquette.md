# Etiquette in Morocco

## Meeting & Greeting Etiquette

* **General Greeting:** Moroccans take their time when greeting, conversing about family, friends, and other general topics.
* **Handshakes:** This is the customary greeting between individuals of the same sex. Handshakes may feel weaker than in Western cultures.
* **Greetings Between Friends:** Once a friendship is established, it is common for people of the same sex to kiss on both cheeks (starting with the left) while shaking hands.
* **Greetings Between Men and Women:** The woman must extend her hand first. If she does not, a man should simply bow his head as a greeting.
* **Entering a Room:** At a social function, start by shaking hands with the person to your right and proceed around the room from right to left.
* **Leaving:** Say goodbye to each person individually before you depart.

---

## Gift Giving Etiquette

* **If Invited to a Home:** It is customary to bring a gift for the hostess, such as sweet pastries, nuts, figs, dates, or flowers.
* **Gifts for Children:** A small gift for the children is appreciated as a token of affection.
* **Alcohol:** Do not bring alcohol as a gift unless you are certain that your host drinks.
* **Opening Gifts:** Gifts are not opened at the time they are received.

---

## Dining Etiquette (When Invited to a Home)

* **Shoes:** You should remove your shoes upon entering the home.
* **Dress Code:** Dress smartly. This is seen as a sign of respect for your hosts.
* **Invitations:** Always check if your spouse is included in the invitation, as some conservative Moroccans may not host mixed-sex groups.
* **Greeting:** Shake hands with everyone present individually.

---

## General Dress Code

* There is no strict dress code, and people can generally wear what they want.
