# Language and Basic Darija Vocabulary in Morocco

## Languages Spoken

* Morocco has two official languages: Arabic and Amazigh.
* The Amazigh language, which uses the Tifinagh alphabet, is considered the common heritage of all Moroccans.
* French and English are also spoken and understood by many Moroccans.
* In the northern and southern regions of Morocco, Spanish is widely spoken.
* The spoken language used in Morocco is Darija, the Moroccan dialect. It is a mixture of Arabic, Amazigh, French, and Spanish.
* Classical or literary Arabic is typically used only in administrative contexts.

Learning a few words in Darija can help you communicate more easily with local inhabitants.
