### The French Protectorate and Modern Capital

The cantoning of Fes within its walls continued until the establishment of the French protectorate in Morocco, which marked a new stage in the urban history of Fes. From this period, a new expansion outside the medina emerged, through the construction of the modern city with its industrial zone, its military base on the Dhar Mehrez plateau, and its railway station on the plateau to the south. The policy of the protectorate, based on segregation between the European population and the local population, resulted in the establishment of separate urban entities: 'the modern city or colonial city' and 'the traditional city or medina.'From the mid-20th century, the city would experience another phase of development with the construction of new neighborhoods on the northern heights of the city. These are Aïn Kadous and Ben Debbab. These two neighborhoods are the result of a new urban policy called "housing for the greatest number," which has led to the creation of the "new Moroccan city."
