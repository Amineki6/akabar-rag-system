### Popular Neighborhoods for Tourists

Fez is divided into three main parts, each offering a distinct experience for tourists. While most visitors spend the majority of their time in the old medina, the other neighborhoods provide a contrast in atmosphere, architecture, and attractions.

Here is an overview of the popular neighborhoods for tourists in Fez:

*** Fes el-Bali (The Old Medina)
This is the heart and soul of Fez and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It's a vast, chaotic, and beautiful medieval labyrinth where most of the city's historical landmarks, artisan workshops, and riad accommodations are located. This car-free zone is where tourists are likely to spend the most time.

Highlights:

*** Historical Attractions: The famous Chouara Tannery, Al-Qarawiyyin Mosque and University, Bou Inania and Al-Attarine Madrasas, and the Mausoleum of Moulay Idriss II are all found here.

*** Souks and Markets: The medina is a dense network of souks (markets), each specializing in a different craft, from leather and ceramics to spices and textiles. It's a fantastic place to shop for authentic Moroccan goods.

Accommodations: This is the best neighborhood for staying in a traditional Moroccan riad. These restored mansions offer a tranquil escape from the bustling streets.

Atmosphere: Fes el-Bali is a sensory overload in the best way possible—filled with the sights, sounds, and smells of traditional life that have changed little over centuries.

*** Fes el-Jdid (The New City)
Founded in the 13th century, Fes el-Jdid is not "new" in a modern sense but serves as a bridge between the ancient medina and the modern city. It is home to significant historical sites and provides a different kind of architectural experience.

Highlights:

*** The Royal Palace: While you cannot enter the palace itself, the exterior and its stunning, ornate golden gates are a major architectural attraction.

*** The Mellah (Jewish Quarter): This historic quarter is known for its distinct architecture, including houses with wooden balconies, and is home to the beautifully restored Ibn Danan Synagogue and a large Jewish Cemetery.

*** Jnan Sbil Gardens: Located near the medina, this beautiful public garden is a tranquil escape with fountains, ponds, and shaded walkways, perfect for a peaceful stroll.

 *** Ville Nouvelle (The New City)
Developed during the French protectorate in the early 20th century, the Ville Nouvelle offers a complete contrast to the medinas. It has wider streets, modern shops, cafes, and restaurants. While it lacks the historical charm of the old city, it is a practical and convenient neighborhood for tourists.

Highlights:

*** Shopping and Dining: This area features modern shops, department stores, and a wider variety of restaurants, including international cuisines. It is a good place to find a more conventional nightlife scene.

*** Hotels: Many of Fez's larger, modern hotels are located here, offering a different kind of accommodation than the riads of the medina.

Convenience: The Ville Nouvelle is where you will find banks, ATM machines, and modern amenities. It is also the main transportation hub with the train station and bus terminal.ride.
