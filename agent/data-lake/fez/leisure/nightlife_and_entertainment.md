## Nightlife & Entertainment in fez

Fez offers a more traditional and subdued nightlife compared to cities like Marrakech or Casablanca. The focus is less on raucous clubs and more on cultural experiences, relaxing lounges, and dining with live entertainment. The nightlife scene is a blend of traditional Moroccan charm and a few modern venues, often found within the larger hotels and stylish riads.

Here's a breakdown of nightlife and entertainment in Fez:

### Cultural & Traditional Experiences

* **Dinner Shows:** Many upscale restaurants and riads offer evening dinner shows that provide an immersive cultural experience. These typically include a multi-course Moroccan meal, often with live performances of traditional music, belly dancing, or folk dancing. These shows are a great way to experience Moroccan hospitality and artistic heritage in a beautiful setting.
* **Traditional Music:** Fez is a center for traditional Moroccan and Sufi music. Some venues host performances of Andalusian orchestras, Sufi chanting (like the Aissawa brotherhood), or rhythmic Gnawa music. These are often held in intimate, candle-lit settings, creating a very special atmosphere.
* **Evening Strolls in the Medina:** The Fez medina, with its labyrinthine alleys, takes on a different, more tranquil character at night. The crowds thin out, and the soft glow of lanterns illuminates the streets. It's a perfect time for a peaceful walk, a cup of mint tea at a small café, or some late-night souvenir shopping at the stalls that stay open.

### Bars and Lounges

While bars and clubs are not as common as in other cities, there are several options, especially in the Ville Nouvelle (the new city) and within luxury hotels.

* **Rooftop Bars:** Many of the most popular evening spots in Fez are rooftop bars, particularly those associated with stylish riads or boutique hotels. They offer a relaxed ambiance, often with ambient music, and spectacular panoramic views of the medina, which is particularly enchanting at sunset as the city lights begin to twinkle.
    * **Mezzanine:** This is a popular spot with a rooftop terrace that offers a relaxed atmosphere and a selection of tapas, drinks, and occasional live music.
    * **Hotel Bars:** The larger, more modern hotels in the Ville Nouvelle are the primary locations for traditional bars and lounges. 

### Nighttime Activities

* **Cooking Classes:** Many guesthouses and tour operators offer evening cooking classes where you can learn to prepare traditional Moroccan dishes. These classes often end with a shared dinner, providing a social and delicious way to spend an evening.
* **Food Stalls and Souks:** The areas around Bab Bou Jeloud and Talaa Kebira in the medina have food stalls that remain active late into the evening. This is a great way to sample local street food and soak up the lively, bustling atmosphere.
* **Relaxing at a Café:** Moroccan culture revolves around socializing in cafés. Evenings are a perfect time to sit in a local café, sip on a sweet mint tea or a coffee, and people-watch as the city winds down.

### Cultural Entertainment

Fez is a city where culture is not just seen, but experienced. The entertainment scene is less about late-night revelry and more about immersive, authentic encounters with Moroccan traditions. From ancient music to hands-on workshops, Fez offers a rich tapestry of cultural activities.

### Music and Performance

* **Traditional Moroccan Music:** Fez is a major hub for traditional music genres, including Andalusian, Malhoun, and Sufi music. Many restaurants and guesthouses host live performances, often in the evening. The music is an integral part of the dining experience, providing a beautiful backdrop of rhythmic and melodic sound.
* **Sufi and Gnawa Music:** For a truly unique and spiritual experience, seek out a performance of Sufi or Gnawa music. Sufi music is often described as mystical and meditative, while Gnawa is known for its trance-inducing rhythms and hypnotic chants. These performances are not just for entertainment but are a significant part of the culture's spiritual traditions. While some performances can be found in small venues, they are most prominent during the city's festivals.
* **Dinner Shows:** Many of the most luxurious riads and restaurants in Fez offer dinner shows that combine a multi-course Moroccan feast with a show featuring dancers, musicians, and storytellers. These are a great option for a curated evening of cultural immersion and fine dining.

### Festivals

Fez is world-renowned for its annual festivals, which are a major draw for tourists and a source of national pride.

* **Fez Festival of World Sacred Music:** Held annually in late May or early June, this festival brings together musicians and artists from around the globe to celebrate the spiritual power of music. Performances take place in historic venues across the city, including the grand Bab Al Makina courtyard. UNESCO has recognized the festival for its contribution to intercultural dialogue.
* **Fez Festival of Sufi Culture:
This festival, usually held in October, is a week-long celebration of the mystical dimensions of Islam. It features a program of concerts, intellectual discussions, and spiritual ceremonies, providing a deep dive into Sufism's message of peace and unity.

***The Amazigh New Year Celebration Festival .This festival is organized by the Aman Association for Sustainable Development in partnership with the Fez-Meknes Regional Council. It celebrates the African dimension of Amazigh culture and reached its third edition in 2018, corresponding to 2968 in the Amazigh calendar.

*** The National Festival of Listening and Praise ArtOrganized by the municipality of Fez in partnership with the Fez-Meknes Regional Council, this event celebrates the arts of listening and praise across the Kingdom. This art is characteristic of cities in the region such as Fez, Meknes, and Taza, and the festival reached its twenty-second edition in 2019.


### Hands-on Workshops and Classes

To truly connect with the local culture, consider a hands-on workshop.

* **Moroccan Cooking Classes:** Fez is a paradise for food lovers, and a cooking class is an excellent way to learn about the city's culinary traditions. Many classes begin with a guided tour of the local souk (market) to select fresh ingredients, followed by a hands-on session where you'll learn to prepare classic dishes like tagine, couscous, or pastilla.
* **Artisan Workshops:** Fez is famous for its traditional crafts. Look for workshops where you can learn about the art of mosaic tile (*zellige*), leatherwork, or pottery. These experiences offer a deeper appreciation for the skills that have been passed down through generations of artisans.
* **Mint Tea Ceremonies:** Learning to prepare Moroccan mint tea is a cultural ritual in itself. Many guesthouses offer a demonstration or a short workshop on the traditional method of making and serving this staple beverage, a cornerstone of Moroccan hospitality.
