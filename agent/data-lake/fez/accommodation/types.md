### Types of Accommodation
Fez offers a diverse range of accommodation options, with each providing a different experience depending on your budget and preference. The choice between staying in the old medina (Fes el-Bali) or the new city (Ville Nouvelle) is often the most important decision for visitors.

### 1. Riads and Dars (The Medina Experience)

This is the most popular and authentic type of accommodation in Fez, offering a unique and immersive stay inside the historic medina. A **riad** is a traditional Moroccan house with a central courtyard or garden. Most riads are beautifully restored mansions with intricate tilework (*zellige*), carved plaster, and an open-air central space. Staying in a riad provides a quiet and elegant escape from the bustling city streets. A **dar** is similar to a riad but is a smaller, traditional home with a courtyard but without a garden. Many have also been converted into guesthouses.

### 2. Hotels (Medina and Ville Nouvelle)

Hotels in Fez vary widely, from small, boutique hotels to large international chains. Some hotels are located inside the medina, offering a more modern alternative to a riad while staying close to the historic sites. In the Ville Nouvelle, you will find most of the large, modern hotels, including international chains. These hotels are located on wide boulevards and offer amenities like swimming pools, bars, and restaurants.

### 3. Hostels (Budget-Friendly)

Fez has a growing number of hostels that cater to backpackers and budget travelers. Most are located in the medina and are often found in small, renovated traditional houses. They offer dormitory-style rooms, communal kitchens, and common areas, providing a social environment for travelers to meet.

### 4. Vacation Rentals 

For those who prefer a self-catering option or are traveling in a group, vacation rentals are a great choice. Websites offer everything from single rooms in a local's house to entire riads or apartments for rent. This option provides more space and privacy.
