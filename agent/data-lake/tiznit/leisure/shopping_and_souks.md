A guide to the souks and markets in Tiznit

The traditional souk of Tiznit

The commercial heart of Tiznit is found within the traditional souk, which stretches from the Méchouar to the historic ramparts. Considered one of the oldest markets in Morocco, it is especially famous for its Berber silver jewelry, handcrafted with ancestral know-how. In addition to jewelry, one can also find slippers, glazed pottery, and rugs woven with traditional geometric patterns.

The province of Tiznit has several weekly rural souks that serve as a crucial economic and social meeting point for the inhabitants of the surrounding mountains and countryside. Prefer cash payments, as the market is essentially cash only. Plan to have small bills or coins for purchases. Be prepared to negotiate politely to get the best price, especially on handcrafted items. Bargaining is a must, but always with respect and good humor. Take the time to observe the artisans at work, as it is an important part of the experience.

Silver jewelry
Tiznit is world-renowned for its finely crafted silver jewelry, which you will find in the jewelers' souk located around the Al-Mahbas square and near the ramparts of the medina. There is a wide selection of silver objects such as brooches, necklaces, filigree bracelets, decorated daggers, pendants, often inlaid with semi-precious stones or enamels.
