# Mohammed V International Airport (CMN)

## Overview

Mohammed V International Airport (`IATA: CMN`, `ICAO: GMMN`) is the busiest airport in Morocco and the primary international gateway to the country. Located in the province of Nouaceur, approximately 30 km south-east of Casablanca, it serves as the main hub for the national carrier, Royal Air Maroc. As a major transit point for flights between Europe, Africa, and North America, CMN is one of the most significant aviation hubs on the African continent.

## Quick Facts

* **Official Name:** Mohammed V International Airport
* **IATA Code:** `CMN`
* **ICAO Code:** `GMMN`
* **Location:** Nouaceur Province, Casablanca-Settat, Morocco
* **Operator:** ONDA (Moroccan Airports Authority)
* **Hub For:** Royal Air Maroc, Air Arabia Maroc
* **Website:** [www.onda.ma](http://www.onda.ma/en/Our-Airports/Casablanca-Mohammed-V-Airport)

## Terminals

The airport currently operates two main passenger terminals: Terminal 1 and Terminal 2.

* **Terminal 1:** This is the newer and more modern terminal. It primarily handles flights operated by Royal Air Maroc and its international partners within the Oneworld alliance.
* **Terminal 2:** This terminal serves a wide range of international airlines, including many European legacy carriers and low-cost airlines.

The terminals are located adjacent to each other and are connected by a walkway, making transfers relatively straightforward. Always check your ticket to confirm which terminal your flight departs from.

## Passenger Services and Facilities

Being a large international hub, the airport offers a comprehensive range of services.

### Dining and Shopping
* A wide variety of restaurants, international fast-food chains (like McDonald's and Starbucks), and cafés are available in both terminals, both pre- and post-security.
* Extensive duty-free shopping is available in the departures lounges, offering a large selection of international and local goods.

### Lounges
* Several VIP and business-class lounges are available, including Royal Air Maroc's "Le Zénith" lounge in Terminal 1.
* Pay-per-use lounges are also accessible to economy passengers for a fee.

### Financial Services
* Multiple currency exchange counters and ATMs are located throughout both terminals in the arrivals and departures areas.

### Other Services
* **Car Rental:** All major international car rental companies (Hertz, Avis, Sixt, Europcar) have desks in the arrivals hall.
* **Wi-Fi:** Free Wi-Fi is provided throughout the airport.
* **Post Office:** A post office is available on-site.
* **Pharmacy:** A pharmacy is located in the public area of the airport.
* **Prayer Rooms:** Dedicated prayer rooms are available.

## Ground Transportation

### Train
* **This is often the most convenient option.** The airport has a train station located on the basement level (-1) of Terminal 1.
* The **ONCF** national railway company operates hourly trains that connect the airport directly to Casablanca's main train stations, **Casa Voyageurs** and **Casa Port**.
* The journey to Casa Voyageurs takes approximately 35-45 minutes. From there, you can connect to the national rail network or the city's tram system.

### Taxis
* **Grand Taxis** (white in Casablanca) are available 24/7 from the taxi rank outside the arrivals hall.
* Fares to the city center are regulated, but it is crucial to agree on the price with the driver before beginning your journey to avoid overcharging. The trip to the city center takes 45-60 minutes, depending on traffic.

### Buses
* The **CTM** bus company operates coach services from the airport to the city center. This is a budget-friendly option, but the schedule may be less frequent than the train.
