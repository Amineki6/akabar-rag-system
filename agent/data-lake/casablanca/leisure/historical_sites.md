### Historic Sites & Viewpoints

* **Hassan II Mosque**
    The city's most breathtaking landmark, the Hassan II Mosque is one of the largest in the world. Its minaret dominates the skyline, and its location on the edge of the Atlantic is stunning. It is one of the few mosques in Morocco open to non-Muslims, who can explore its exquisite interior craftsmanship through guided tours.

* **The Habous Quarter (New Medina)**
    A unique district built by the French in the 1930s, the Habous Quarter blends traditional Moroccan architecture with European planning. Its clean, arched alleyways house artisanal shops, book sellers, and traditional souks. Key sights within the quarter include the impressive **Mahkama Du Pacha** and the **Royal Palace**.

* **Art Deco District**
    Casablanca's city center is a living museum of Art Deco and Mauresque (Moorish revival) architecture. A walk around **Mohammed V Square** reveals masterpieces from the French Protectorate era, including the Wilaya (City Hall), the Palace of Justice, and the iconic Grande Poste (Main Post Office).

* **Old Medina**
    The small, walled Old Medina offers a look at what Casablanca was like before the French era. Its labyrinthine streets, historic ramparts, and bustling souks provide an authentic, unfiltered local experience.

* **The Corniche (Ain Diab)**
    The coastal promenade of Ain Diab serves as a major city landmark, offering sweeping views of the Atlantic. It is lined with beaches, cafes, and restaurants, making it a focal point for leisure and recreation. The **El Hank Lighthouse**, at its western end, stands as a historic maritime beacon.
