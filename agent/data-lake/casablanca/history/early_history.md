### Early History

 The current site of Casablanca was inhabited by humans during the Paleolithic era. The origins of the city are not exactly known, but it seems that the city of Anfa was once located in the same place as it is today.Archaeological discoveries at Sidi Abderrahman (the south exit of Casablanca) attest to the settlement of the site since prehistoric times. It appears that Anfa was inhabited by amazigh  fishermen from the earliest antiquity, a time when the place served as a stopover for Phoenician ships en route to the Purpurarius Islands off Essaouira. By 744 AD, the city had become a significant part of the Berber kingdom of Barghawata. The historian Leo Africanus described Anfa as the most "prosperous city on the Atlantic Coast because of its fertile land."

In 1068, the Almoravids conquered the region, ending the independence of the Barghawata. Following this, Arab tribes of Hilal and Sulaym descent settled in the area, leading to widespread Arabization through intermingling with the local Berber population. Under the Merinid dynasty in the 14th century, Anfa's importance as a port grew. However, the Merinids were eventually ousted by a popular revolt in 1465.
