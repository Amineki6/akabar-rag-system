### Where to Eat

#### Marché Central (Central Market)
For an authentic taste of the city's seafood, the Central Market is an unmissable destination. The bustling atmosphere, combined with the absolute freshness of the fish which you can select yourself for grilling, makes for a memorable and delicious lunch.

#### The Corniche (Ain Diab)
This seaside promenade is lined with a wide range of dining options, from upscale restaurants offering international cuisine and fine dining to trendy beach clubs and cafes with stunning ocean views. It's the perfect place for a more luxurious or celebratory meal.

#### Habous Quarter
The city's "New Medina" offers a more traditional and charming dining experience. Here you can find classic Moroccan restaurants, as well as excellent bakeries and patisseries selling traditional Moroccan sweets and pastries.

#### Gauthier and Maarif
These modern, central neighborhoods are known for their vibrant and diverse food scenes. They are packed with trendy cafes, bistros, and international restaurants offering everything from French and Italian to Asian cuisine, catering to the city's cosmopolitan residents.

#### Old Medina
The alleyways of the old medina are dotted with small, no-frills eateries and street food stalls offering some of the most authentic and affordable food in the city. It's a great place to grab a quick, traditional bite while exploring.
