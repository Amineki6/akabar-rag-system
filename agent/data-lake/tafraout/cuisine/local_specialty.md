Regional Specialties & Signature Dishes
 
* ** Tagine
This slow-cooked stew is a national dish, and in tafraout, the Fish Tagine (Tagine de Poisson) is a local specialty. This version often features firm local fish like monkfish (lotte) or conger eel, cooked with tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, and a flavorful 'chermoula' marinade. You will also find classic variations with lamb or chicken, often simmered with preserved lemon and olives.

* **Amlou
A delicious and energizing spread that is a true specialty of the Souss region. It is a thick, brown paste made from roasted almonds, pure culinary Argan oil, and honey. It's typically served for breakfast or as a snack and used as a dip for bread.
 
 
* **Culinary Argan Oil (Huile d'Argan Alimentaire)
The Argan tree is endemic to the region around Agadir, making its culinary oil a fundamental local ingredient. It has a distinct, nutty flavor from roasting the kernels and is used as a finishing oil drizzled over salads and tagines, or simply as a dip for bread.
 
* **Tafarnout Bread
A large, round, and somewhat flat traditional Amazigh (Berber) bread known for its chewy texture and rustic flavor. It is traditionally baked on a bed of hot stones or in a communal clay oven and is the perfect vessel for dipping in tagine, Argan oil, or Amlou.
 
* **Tagoula
A hearty and simple Berber porridge made from barley semolina. It is considered a staple of Amazigh cuisine in the region and is typically served in a large communal dish with a well created in the center, which is then filled with Argan oil, Amlou, or smen (fermented butter) for dipping.
 
* **Couscous
The national dish of Morocco, often served with vegetables and meat. Friday is traditionally "couscous day," so you'll find it fresh and abundant on this day.
 
* **Harira
A hearty, traditional Moroccan soup made with lentils, chickpeas, and tomatoes. It is often served with a soft-boiled egg, dates, and a sweet chebakia cookie.
* **Idernan
Idernan désigne une trêve saisonnière ancestrale instaurée entre les tribus du Souss, symbole d’une paix tribale célébrée par des fêtes traditionnelles favorisant la confiance et la coexistence sans armes. L'Idernan est aussi  une crêpe berbère festive typique des montagnes de l'Anti-Atlas au Maroc, préparée à base de semoule et farine, symbolisant la convivialité amazighe lors des célébrations culturelles.
* **berkouks ou le couscous avec amlou
Le Berkoukes est un couscous à gros grains fabriqué à partir de semoule de blé dur, roulée traditionnellement à la main en minuscules grains. Plat ancestral d’origine amazigh, il est particulièrement apprécié dans le sud du Maroc, notamment à Tafraoute. Sa texture dense et ses grains tendres, cuits à la vapeur, en font un mets réconfortant.
Ce plat incarne la richesse gastronomique du peuple amazigh. Le Berkoukes et son accompagnement à l'Amlou sont plus qu’une recette : c’est un héritage transmis de génération en génération, symbole de convivialité, de lien social, et de fierté culturelle à Tafraoute et dans tout le Maroc. Lors des fêtes et réunions familiales, il rassemble autour d’un repas généreux et chaleureux.
* ** Ourkimn
 est un plat traditionnel amazigh riche en histoire et en symbolisme, préparé spécialement à l'occasion du Nouvel An amazigh (Yennayer), symbolisant la cohésion sociale et la continuité des traditions agricoles et culturelles.
