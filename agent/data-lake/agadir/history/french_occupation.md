### The French Protectorate and Modern Development

French troops landed in Agadir in 1913. Under the French protectorate, the city saw its first modern development. A port was constructed, and new districts like the old **Talborjt** and **Yahchech** were built. Around 1930, Agadir became an important stop for the French airmail service **Aéropostale**, frequented by famous aviators like Saint-Exupéry. A modern city center began to take shape based on the plans of urban planners Henri Prost and Michel Ecochard.

By the 1950s, with the opening of a new commercial port, the city grew rapidly, driven by fishing, canning, agriculture, and mining. It also began to establish itself as a tourist destination.
