### Where to Eat

#### Djemaa el-Fna Food Stalls
As dusk falls, the main square transforms into an enormous open-air restaurant. Dozens of numbered stalls are set up, each offering a different specialty. This is the best place to experience the energy of the city while trying a variety of street foods, from grilled merguez sausages and kebabs to tagines, fried fish, and local specialties like snail soup or sheep's head.

#### Medina Restaurants & Riads
The narrow alleyways of the old city are home to countless traditional restaurants. Many riads also have their own restaurants, which are often open to non-guests with a reservation. These offer a more refined and intimate dining experience, serving high-quality, authentic Moroccan cuisine in a beautiful and tranquil courtyard setting.

#### Gueliz (The New City)
For a change of pace, the modern neighborhood of Gueliz offers a wide selection of contemporary restaurants, international cuisine, and trendy cafes. If you're looking for French bistros, Italian restaurants, or just a modern coffee shop, this is the place to go.

#### The Souks
Beyond the main square, the souks are filled with small, hidden eateries and stalls where locals eat. These hole-in-the-wall spots offer some of the most authentic and affordable food in the city. It's here you can find a freshly baked 'khobz' (bread) or a delicious bowl of soup for lunch.
