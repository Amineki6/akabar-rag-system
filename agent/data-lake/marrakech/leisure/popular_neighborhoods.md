### Popular Neighborhoods for Tourists

#### The Medina
The Medina is the historic heart of Marrakech, a labyrinth of narrow alleyways, bustling souks, and historical landmarks. Staying here means you are within walking distance of major attractions like Djemaa el-Fna square and the Koutoubia Mosque. It's the best area for a culturally immersive experience, with a high concentration of riads. The Medina itself is comprised of several distinct quarters:
* **Kasbah:** Located in the southern part of the Medina, this historic royal district offers a slightly less hectic atmosphere. It's home to the Saadian Tombs and El Badi Palace.
* **Mouassine:** Known for its stylish riads and proximity to the souks, this central area has a blend of history and artistry.
* **Mellah (Jewish Quarter):** Situated in the southeastern part of the Medina, this historic Jewish quarter is quieter and close to the Bahia Palace.

#### Gueliz
Gueliz is the modern "new city" of Marrakech, built by the French. It features wide avenues, contemporary art galleries, trendy cafes, and international brand shopping. This neighborhood is ideal for those who prefer a more European feel, with modern hotels and apartments. It offers a vibrant nightlife and a less chaotic environment than the Medina.

#### Hivernage
Known as the chic and upscale district of Marrakech, Hivernage is characterized by its luxury hotels, high-end restaurants, and palm-lined avenues. It has a more tranquil, resort-like feel and is a great option for travelers looking for a lavish and relaxing stay while still being relatively close to the Medina.

#### Palmeraie
Located a short drive north of the city, the Palmeraie is a vast palm grove that offers a peaceful and luxurious retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. This area is dominated by sprawling resorts, opulent villas, and all-inclusive hotels. It's an ideal choice for a romantic getaway or a relaxing family vacation, with activities like camel rides through the palm groves.
