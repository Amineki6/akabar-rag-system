Marrakech has a rich history of religious and spiritual life, with a number of significant sites that are popular with both locals and tourists.

Here are some of the most notable religious sites in Marrakech:

### Mosques and Islamic Sites

* **Koutoubia Mosque:** This is the largest and most famous mosque in Marrakech, and its towering minaret is a major landmark of the city. While non-Muslims are not permitted to enter the mosque itself, you can admire its impressive architecture from the outside and stroll through the surrounding gardens.
* **Ben Youssef Madrasa:** This former Islamic college, or madrasa, is a masterpiece of Marinid and Saadian architecture. Although it is no longer an active school, its intricately decorated courtyards, prayer hall, and student dormitories are a must-see for anyone interested in Islamic art and history.
* **Kasbah Mosque:** Located in the old royal quarter of the city, this mosque is known for its beautiful and imposing minaret, which is often compared to that of the Koutoubia Mosque.
* **The "Seven Saints" of Marrakech (Sabaatou Rijal):** Marrakech is known for its veneration of a group of seven saints, whose tombs (or *zaouias*) are scattered throughout the city. This tradition is a significant part of the city's spiritual heritage, and the pilgrimage to these sites is a major event for many Moroccans. The seven saints are:
    * Sidi Youssef Ben Ali
    * Qadi Ayyad
    * Sidi Bel Abbas
    * Sidi Suleiman Al Jazuli
    * Sidi Abdel Aziz
    * Sidi Abdullah Ghazouani
    * Imam Souhaili

### Other Religious Sites

* **The Mellah (Jewish Quarter):** The historic Jewish quarter of Marrakech, the Mellah, is a testament to the city's religious diversity. It is home to a number of synagogues, though many are no longer active. The **Slat Al Azama Synagogue** is one of the most well-known and is a place of historical and cultural significance.
* **Church of the Holy Martyrs:** This is a Catholic church located in the modern part of Marrakech, Gueliz. It serves the city's Christian community.
