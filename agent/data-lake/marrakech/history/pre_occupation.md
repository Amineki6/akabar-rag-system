### Almohad Golden Age

In 1147, Marrakech was conquered by the **Almohads**, a rival amazigh  dynasty. They initiated a massive rebuilding program, destroying many Almoravid structures they deemed impure but also embarking on their own ambitious architectural projects. This period is considered a golden age for the city. The Almohads made Marrakech their capital and constructed some of its most enduring landmarks, including the iconic **Koutoubia Mosque**, the Menara Gardens, and extensive expansions to the city walls and the Kasbah.

---

### Decline and Saadian Renaissance

Following the fall of the Almohads, Marrakech entered a period of relative decline under the **Marinid dynasty**, which favored Fes as its capital. However, the city's fortunes were revived in the 16th century when it became the capital of the **Saadian dynasty**. The Saadians restored the city's former glory, building magnificent palaces such as the opulent **El Badi Palace**. They also created the lavish **Saadian Tombs** to serve as their royal necropolis, a site whose splendor would remain hidden from the world until its rediscovery in 1917.

---

### Alaouite Rule 

The **Alaouite dynasty**, which still rules Morocco today, took control of Marrakech in the 17th century. The early Alaouite sultans, particularly **Moulay Ismail**, focused their attention on their new imperial capital at Meknes. The city remained an important regional center but lost its status as the primary seat of power for long periods.
