# Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport (TNG)

## Overview

Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport (`IATA: TNG`, `ICAO: GMTT`) is the international airport serving Tangier, the major city and economic hub of northern Morocco. Named after the famous 14th-century Moroccan scholar and explorer who was born in the city, the airport is strategically located near the Strait of Gibraltar. It caters to a growing mix of business travelers, tourists exploring the northern coast, and members of the Moroccan diaspora. The airport has undergone significant modernization to handle increasing passenger traffic, reflecting Tangier's rising importance as an industrial and logistical center.

## Quick Facts

* **Official Name:** Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport
* **IATA Code:** `TNG`
* **ICAO Code:** `GMTT`
* **Location:** Boukhalef, approximately 12 km (7.5 miles) southwest of Tangier city center.
* **Operator:** ONDA (Moroccan Airports Authority)
* **Website:** [www.onda.ma](http://www.onda.ma/en/Our-Airports/Tanger-Ibn-Battouta-Airport)

The airport consists of **one modern passenger terminal** that handles all domestic and international flights. The terminal is a single, well-organized building that was renovated and expanded to provide a comfortable and efficient experience for travelers. Its manageable size makes it easy to navigate from check-in to the boarding gates.

The terminal is equipped with all the necessary amenities for international travelers.

## Ground Transportation

Getting from the airport to the city center is straightforward, with taxis being the most common option.

### Taxis
* **Grand Taxis** (large, cream-colored sedans) are the primary method for getting to the city. They are available at the official taxi rank directly outside the arrivals hall.
* Fares to the city center and the port area are generally fixed. It is always best to confirm the price with the driver before starting your journey. The trip usually takes around 20-30 minutes, depending on traffic.

### Car Rental
* Renting a car is a popular choice for visitors planning to explore the northern region of Morocco, including destinations like Asilah, Chefchaouen, and Tétouan. The rental desks are conveniently located in the arrivals hall.

### Private Transfers
* Many hotels and private companies offer pre-booked transfer services. This is a convenient, hassle-free option where a driver will meet you in the arrivals hall.

### Renting a Car

Renting a car offers the freedom to explore Tangier and the beautiful surrounding northern coast and Rif Mountains at your own pace.

#### Booking Process
Rental agencies are available at **Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport (TNG)** and in the city center. To secure the best rates and ensure vehicle availability, especially during the summer, it is highly recommended to book online in advance.

* **Requirements:**
    * A valid driving license from your country of residence is required.
    * An International Driving Permit (IDP) may be requested by some agencies.
    * Drivers usually need to be at least 21 years old and have a valid credit card.

#### Expected Costs
Costs vary based on the season, vehicle type, and rental duration.

* **Economy (e.g., Renault Clio):** €25 - €40 per day
* **Compact SUV (e.g., Dacia Duster):** €40 - €65 per day
* **Luxury:** €90+ per day

#### Insurance Guide
Standard rentals typically include basic third-party liability insurance, but often with a significant excess (deductible).

* **Common Options:**
    * **Collision Damage Waiver (CDW):** This limits your financial liability for any damage done to the rental car.
    * **Personal Accident Insurance (PAI):** This covers medical costs for the driver and passengers. Check if your personal travel insurance already includes this.
* **Pro Tip:** Check with your credit card company before you travel. Many offer rental car insurance as a benefit, which could save you from purchasing it separately.

#### Driving in Tangier
Driving in Tangier can be challenging due to its hilly terrain, narrow streets in older districts, and often heavy traffic.

* Drive on the **right side of the road**.
* Be cautious and assertive, especially at roundabouts and in congested areas.
* Parking can be difficult to find in the city center; paid lots are the most convenient option.

### Intercity Buses

This station connects Tangier to other cities.

#### Gare Routière de Tanger (Tangier Main Bus Station)
* **Location:** Tangier has multiple bus terminals. The main "Gare Routière" is located in the Gueznaia area on the outskirts of the city, approximately 10 km southeast of the medina.
* **Description:** This is the primary bus station for most intercity bus companies, connecting Tangier to a wide variety of destinations across Morocco, including Chefchaouen, Fes, and Marrakech.
* **Note:** CTM, a major bus company, also has its own dedicated bus station closer to the city center and the train station. It is a good idea to confirm which station your bus departs from when you book your ticket. It is also highly recommended to buy tickets in advance for popular routes, especially during peak travel periods.


### Gare de Tanger-Ville (Tangier Train Station)

* **Location:** Situated in the modern part of Tangier, near the city center and the main boulevard, Boulevard Mohammed V. It is also close to the new port and the city's beach.
* **Description:** This is Tangier's main and only passenger train station. It is a modern, spacious, and well-equipped terminal. The station was significantly renovated and expanded to serve as the northern terminus for the high-speed rail line, Al Boraq. It features contemporary architecture, a large waiting hall, shops, cafes (including international brands like Starbucks and McDonald's), and ticket offices. There's a special lounge for Al Boraq first-class passengers.
* **Key Destinations:** Gare de Tanger-Ville is the starting point for the high-speed Al Boraq line, connecting Tangier to the major cities of the Atlantic coast. Key destinations include:
    * **Kenitra** (high-speed)
    * **Rabat** (high-speed)
    * **Casablanca** (high-speed)
    * **Marrakech** (conventional train, often requiring a transfer in Casablanca)
    * **Fez** and **Meknes** (conventional train)
* **Note:** The station's central location makes it a convenient gateway for travelers. From the station, you can easily get a taxi to the medina, the Kasbah, or your hotel. Its direct connection to the high-speed rail network makes it a crucial link for travelers coming from or heading to central and southern Morocco.

### Overview of Transportation in Tangier

The provided documents describe the transportation network in Tangier, which is served by the Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport (TNG), located 12 km from the city center. Ground transport from the airport is primarily handled by Grand Taxis, which have fixed fares to the city.

For travel to other Moroccan cities, the main intercity bus terminal (Gare Routière) is located on the outskirts of the city, though some companies like CTM have their own separate stations. ### Taxis in Tangier

Taxis are a ubiquitous and essential part of getting around Tangier. It's important to distinguish between the two types.

#### Petit Taxi (Small Taxi)
These are the standard taxis for trips within the Tangier city limits.
* **Color:** A distinctive light, bright blue.
* **Scope:** Restricted to trips within the city only.
* **Pricing:** Petit taxis are metered, and you should insist the driver uses the meter. Fares start at a base rate, with most trips within the city costing between 15 and 40 MAD.
* **Passengers:** A maximum of three.

#### Grand Taxi (Large Taxi)
Grand taxis are used for longer journeys, such as to the airport, the Tanger Med port, or other towns like Asilah.
* **Color:** Typically cream or white, often older Mercedes models.
* **Scope:** Authorized for suburban and inter-city trips.
* **Pricing:** They do not use meters. Fares are fixed for specific routes but should always be agreed upon before starting your journey. They can be hired privately or as a shared taxi, which is more economical.
* **Passengers:** Up to six, plus the driver.

## Activities & Experiences in Tangier

Tangier, the gateway between Africa and Europe, offers a unique blend of cultures, history, and natural beauty. From exploring ancient fortresses and mythological caves to relaxing on Mediterranean beaches, the city provides a rich variety of activities and experiences.

---

### Historic & Cultural Sites

* **Tangier Casbah**: Explore the ancient fortress of the city, with its narrow, winding streets, historic gates, and stunning views over the Strait of Gibraltar. Within the Kasbah, you can visit the **Musee de la Kasbah**.
* **Petit Socco**: Immerse yourself in the bustling atmosphere of this historic square in the heart of the Medina.
* **Place du Grand 9 Avril 1947**: A major square in the city that connects the Medina with the Ville Nouvelle, often referred to as the Grand Socco.
* **St. Andrew's Church**: Visit this unique Anglican church that features a blend of English and Moroccan architectural styles, including a Lord's Prayer inscription in Arabic.
* **Moshe Nahon Synagogue**: Discover this beautifully restored 19th-century synagogue in the Medina, showcasing the city's rich Jewish heritage.
* **Gran Mezquita de Tanger**: Admire the architecture of Tangier's Grand Mosque, a key religious landmark in the city.
* **Terrasse des Paresseux**: Enjoy panoramic views of the port and the Strait of Gibraltar from this famous viewpoint, a perfect spot for people-watching.
* **Tomb of Ibn Battouta**: Visit the reputed resting place of the famous 14th-century Moroccan explorer, Ibn Battouta. You can also explore his memory at the **Borj A-na'am - Memories Of Ibn Battouta** museum.
* **Tumbas Fenicias**: Discover ancient Phoenician tombs carved into a rock face, offering a glimpse into the city's distant past.


### Coastal Attractions & Beaches

* **Caves of Hercules**: Visit this famous archaeological cave complex, part natural and part man-made, known for its sea opening shaped like the map of Africa.
* **Cap Spartel**: Stand at the dramatic northwestern tip of Africa, where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea, and see its historic lighthouse.
* **Plage de Tanger**: Relax or take a stroll along the city's main municipal beach.
* **Other Beaches**: Explore nearby beautiful beaches such as **Dalia Beach**, **Ba Kacem Beach**, **Playa Blanca**, and **Bouhendia Beach**.
* **Punta Malabata**: Visit this headland for excellent views of the city and the Strait of Gibraltar.

### Modern & Leisure Activities

* **Shopping Malls**: For a modern shopping experience, visit **Tanger City Mall** or **Ibn Batouta Mall**.
* **Socco Alto**: A modern shopping center offering a variety of brands and dining options.
* **Tanjazz**: If your visit aligns, experience the city's annual international jazz festival.

### Historic Sites & Viewpoints

* **Tangier Casbah**
    The ancient fortress and walled residential quarter at the highest point of the city. Its narrow, winding streets, historic gates, and defensive walls offer a glimpse into the city's past. The Kasbah provides stunning panoramic views of the city, the port, and the Strait of Gibraltar.

* **Caves of Hercules**
    A famous archaeological cave complex located just west of the city. Part natural and part man-made, the cave is steeped in mythology, believed to be the resting place of the Greek hero Hercules. Its most iconic feature is the sea opening, which is shaped like the continent of Africa.

* **Cap Spartel**
    The dramatic headland that marks the northwesternmost point of mainland Africa. It is where the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea converge. The site features a historic lighthouse and offers breathtaking views, especially at sunset.

* **Petit Socco**
    A bustling and atmospheric square located in the heart of the Medina. For centuries, it has been a vibrant hub of city life, surrounded by historic cafes that were once frequented by famous writers and artists of the Beat Generation.

* **Tangier American Legation Museum**
    The only U.S. National Historic Landmark located in a foreign country. This building was the first American public property abroad, serving as a diplomatic mission. Today, it is a cultural center and museum dedicated to the history of the relationship between the United States and Morocco, featuring an impressive collection of art, including works by James McBey.

*** The Church of the Immaculate Conception, also known as the Spanish Church or La Purísima,[1] is a Roman Catholic church built in 1880–1881 in Tangier, Morocco.

*** Bab Al Bahr is one of the historic gates of Tangier, Morocco. It separates the Kasbah of the old city from the sea to the north and is represented by an arch surmounted by a balcony with openings.

### Kasbah Museum of Mediterranean Cultures

Housed within the historic walls of the former sultan's palace, Dar al-Makhzen, the Kasbah Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the rich history of the region. The museum's collections span from prehistoric times to the 19th century, showcasing a diverse range of artifacts that tell the story of Tangier and the broader Mediterranean.

* **Opening Hours:** Monday to Sunday, 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays).
* **Ticket Price:** A combined ticket for the Kasbah Museum and the Museum of Contemporary Art is available. Prices are approximately 20 MAD for Moroccans and residents, and 70 MAD for foreign tourists.

### Tangier American Legation Museum

A unique piece of American history on foreign soil, the Tangier American Legation Museum is the only U.S. National Historic Landmark located in another country. Housed in a beautifully preserved 19th-century building in the heart of the medina, the museum commemorates the long-standing diplomatic and cultural relationship between the United States and Morocco.

* **Opening Hours:** Monday to Friday, 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM; Saturday, 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM (Closed on Sundays).
* **Ticket Price:** 50 MAD for adults, with discounts available for students and children.

### Ibn Battouta Museum

Dedicated to the life and extraordinary journeys of the 14th-century Tangier-born explorer, the Ibn Battouta Museum is an inspiring tribute to one of history's greatest travelers. The museum takes visitors on a captivating journey through the vast territories that Ibn Battouta explored, from North Africa to the far reaches of Asia.

* **Opening Hours:** Monday to Saturday, 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
* **Ticket Price:** Approximately 20 MAD.

### Villa Harris Museum

Opened in 2021, the Villa Harris Museum is a beautiful addition to Tangier's cultural scene. Housed in the stunning former residence of the British journalist Walter Burton Harris, the museum is set within a vast and picturesque park. The collection focuses on the history of art in Morocco, with a particular emphasis on the 20th century.

* **Opening Hours:** Wednesday to Monday, 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays).
* **Ticket Price:** Admission was initially free, with a nominal fee anticipated in the future. It is advisable to check for the most current information upon visiting.

## Nightlife & Entertainment in Tangier

Tangier's nightlife is as diverse and captivating as its history, offering a unique blend of European-style bars, historic cafes, and atmospheric rooftops. The city's entertainment scene reflects its past as an international crossroads, with something to suit every mood, from a quiet, contemplative evening to a lively night out.

---

### Rooftop Bars & Terraces

One of the most magical ways to spend an evening in Tangier is at a rooftop bar. Many riads and hotels in the Kasbah and Medina have converted their terraces into chic lounges. These spots offer breathtaking panoramic views of the city lights, the bustling port, and the distant coastline of Spain across the Strait of Gibraltar, providing a perfect setting for a sunset drink.

### Modern Bars & Clubs

The Ville Nouvelle (New City) and the beachfront are the main hubs for modern nightlife.

* **Ville Nouvelle**: The area around Boulevard Pasteur is filled with a variety of venues, from Spanish-inspired tapas bars to sophisticated cocktail lounges and English-style pubs. It's a popular area for both locals and expatriates to enjoy a drink and socialize.
* **The Beachfront and Marina**: Along the coast, you'll find a growing number of trendy beach clubs and modern bars offering music and a lively atmosphere with a view of the Mediterranean.

### Historic Cafes

A quintessential Tangier experience is spending time in one of its legendary cafes. These are not places for alcohol, but for sipping mint tea and soaking in history.

* **Café Hafa**: A famous cliffside cafe with terraced seating offering stunning sea views. It has been a beloved spot for generations of writers and artists, including the Beatles and the Rolling Stones.
* **Petit Socco**: The cafes in this historic square were once the center of the city's social and literary scene, frequented by figures of the Beat Generation like William S. Burroughs and Allen Ginsberg.

### Live Music & Festivals

Tangier has a vibrant cultural scene with several venues offering live music, ranging from traditional Moroccan sounds to jazz and flamenco. The city also hosts the renowned **Tanjazz** festival, an annual event that fills the city with performances by international and local jazz artists.

### Popular Neighborhoods for Tourists

#### The Kasbah
Perched at the highest point of the Medina, the Kasbah is the historic fortress of the city. Staying here offers an exclusive and tranquil experience, with stunning views across the port and a labyrinth of clean, narrow alleys to explore. It's home to some of the most beautiful and upscale boutique guesthouses and riads.

#### The Medina
The historic heart of Tangier, the Medina is a vibrant maze of narrow streets, bustling souks, and lively squares like the Petit Socco. Staying within the Medina walls places you in the middle of the action, close to traditional restaurants, artisan shops, and many of the city's historic sights. It's ideal for those seeking a deeply immersive cultural experience.

#### Ville Nouvelle (New City)
The "New City," built during the 20th century, offers a stark contrast to the old town with its wide boulevards, European architecture, and modern amenities. This area, centered around the Place de France and Boulevard Pasteur, has a high concentration of hotels, international restaurants, cafes, and shops. It's a convenient and practical base for exploring the entire city.

#### The Beachfront (Avenue Mohammed VI)
The area along Tangier's main city beach and the redeveloped Tanja Marina Bay is perfect for those who want to be close to the sea. This wide bay is lined with large hotels, modern apartments, beach clubs, and seafood restaurants. It offers a resort-like feel and beautiful views of the Mediterranean.

Tangier is a city with a rich history and a diverse religious landscape, reflected in its mosques, churches, and synagogues. Here are some of the key religious sites and attractions to visit:

### Mosques

* **Grand Mosque of Tangier:** Located in the city's old medina, this historic mosque has a long and fascinating history. The site has been used for religious purposes since antiquity, originally housing a Roman temple and later a Roman church. It was converted to a mosque by the Marinid dynasty and later rebuilt by Sultan Moulay Slimane in the 19th century. As with most mosques in Morocco, it is generally not open to non-Muslims. However, its beautiful exterior and minaret, decorated with traditional geometric motifs and green tiles, are worth seeing.
* **Kasbah Mosque:** Situated in the Kasbah (the fortified part of the medina), this mosque is another important historical and architectural site.
* **Sidi Bou Abib Mosque:** Built in 1917, this mosque is known for its distinctive Mauresque style and colorful minaret, which is a prominent landmark in the Grand Socco square.

### Churches

* **St. Andrew's Church:** This Anglican church is a charming and historically significant site. Consecrated in 1905, it features a unique blend of architectural styles, including a minaret-inspired bell tower and horseshoe arches. The churchyard is also notable, serving as the final resting place for many prominent figures from Tangier's international past.
* **Roman Catholic Cathedral of Tangier (Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception and the Holy Spirit):** Also known as the Spanish Cathedral, this modernist building was completed in 1961. Its bell tower was once the tallest structure in the city.
* **Church of the Immaculate Conception:** Located in the medina, this Spanish-built church dates back to the late 19th century.
* **French Church of Tangier:** This church is another testament to the city's diverse European influences.

### Synagogues

* **Moshe Nahon Synagogue:** This is one of the last remaining operating synagogues in Tangier and a significant site for understanding the city's once-thriving Jewish community. Located on Rue Synagogue, it is known for its lavish interior with intricate carvings and artifacts.

## A Guide to Souks and Shopping in Tangier

Shopping in Tangier is a unique experience, reflecting the city's history as an international crossroads. From the winding alleys of the historic Medina, filled with traditional bazaars, to the modern boutiques of the Ville Nouvelle, the city offers a diverse retail landscape that blends Moroccan craftsmanship with European style.

---

### Traditional Souks & Bazaars

The heart of traditional shopping in Tangier is its vibrant Medina and Kasbah. Unlike the vast, single-enclosed souks of other Moroccan cities, Tangier's markets are a web of specialized streets and bustling squares.

**Key Areas:**
* **The Grand Socco (Place du 9 Avril 1947)**: This large, bustling square serves as the main entrance to Medina. While it's more of a transport hub, it's surrounded by shops and marks the beginning of the market streets. It also hosts a lively open-air market with fresh produce.
* **The Petit Socco**: Deep within Medina, this smaller, atmospheric square is surrounded by artisan shops and historic cafes. The streets radiating from it are filled with shops selling traditional goods.
***Rue es-Siaghin & Rue de la Marine**: These are two of the main shopping arteries within Medina. Here you can find a dense concentration of bazaars selling a wide array of goods, including leather products (bags, shoes), kaftans, textiles, spices, and souvenirs.
* **Specialized Markets**: Within the Medina, you'll find areas dedicated to specific crafts, including a weavers' market and areas for metalworkers and other artisans.

**Visitor Tips:**
* **Bargaining is expected** in most shops within the Medina. It's a respectful part of the transaction.
* While still lively, the souks in Tangier are often considered less hectic than those in Marrakech or Fes.
* Don't hesitate to explore the smaller side streets, as they often hide unique artisan workshops.

---

### Modern Shopping

For a contemporary retail experience with fixed prices, head to the Ville Nouvelle (New City).

#### Ville Nouvelle (New City)
* **Boulevard Pasteur**: This is the main commercial thoroughfare of modern Tangier. The street and its surrounding area are lined with a mix of international brands, Spanish-style boutiques, perfumeries, bookshops, and elegant patisseries.
* **Tanger City Mall**: For a complete mall experience, Tanger City Mall, located near the beachfront, offers several floors of international and Moroccan retail chains, a large supermarket, a food court, and a cinema.

### Regional Specialties & Signature Dishes

#### Fresh Seafood & Fish
Given its prime location on the Strait of Gibraltar, Tangier offers an abundance of incredibly fresh seafood. A must-try experience is visiting the fish market or a portside restaurant to enjoy the day's catch. Common preparations include grilled fish, fried calamari and shrimp, and 'pil-pil' shrimp (prawns cooked in olive oil, garlic, and chili).

#### Bissara
A hearty and traditional fava bean soup, particularly popular in northern Morocco. It is slow-cooked until creamy, then drizzled with olive oil and sprinkled with cumin and paprika. Often eaten for breakfast, it's a staple of Tangier's working-class diet and is found in many small eateries in the Medina.

#### Tagine
The classic Moroccan slow-cooked stew is ubiquitous in Tangier. While all traditional variations are available, the **Fish Tagine (Tagine de Poisson)** is a local favorite, often prepared with a flavorful 'chermoula' marinade, tomatoes, and olives.

#### Bocadillos & Tortillas
Tangier's proximity to Spain is evident in its food. Spanish-style **bocadillos** (sandwiches in baguette-like bread) are a popular street food. Many cafes and restaurants also serve a Moroccan-style **tortilla**, similar to a Spanish omelet but often thicker and filled with potatoes and onions.

#### Harira
The traditional Moroccan soup, made from a rich base of tomatoes, lentils, and chickpeas, is a staple in Tangier. It's a comforting and flavorful dish found in restaurants throughout the city.

#### Couscous
The national dish of Morocco, consisting of steamed semolina grains served with a stew of vegetables and meat, is widely available. Friday is the traditional day for couscous, and many restaurants feature it as a special.

#### Mint Tea (Thé à la Menthe)
A cornerstone of Moroccan hospitality, mint tea is served everywhere. In Tangier, it's often enjoyed in the city's many historic cafes, where you can sip your tea while watching the world go by.

### Ancient Origins: Tingis

Tangier is one of North Africa's most ancient cities. According to amazigh  mythology, it was founded by a hero named Sufax, and Greek mythology links its name to the giant Antaeus. ### The International Zone (1923-1956)

In the early 20th century, as European influence in Morocco grew, Tangier's unique status was formalized. In 1923, it was designated an **International Zone**, governed by a commission of foreign powers including France, Spain, and Britain (later joined by others, including the USA and Italy).

This period gave Tangier a legendary reputation. It became a tax haven and a hub for espionage, smuggling, and unchecked freedom. The city attracted a colorful cast of expatriates, including writers like Paul Bowles, Truman Capote, and William S. Burroughs, as well as artists, socialites, and spies, all drawn by its libertine atmosphere.

### Islamic Conquest and Dynastic Rule

After the fall of the Roman Empire, Tangier came under the control of the Vandals and then the Byzantine Empire. In the early eighth century, it was conquered by the Umayyad Arab forces led by **Musa bin Nusayr**. The city served as a crucial staging ground for the Tariq ibn Ziyad's invasion and subsequent conquest of the Iberian Peninsula in 711. For the next several centuries, Tangier was ruled by a succession of Berber and Arab dynasties, including the Idrisids, Almoravids, and Almohads.

---

### European Contention (1471-1844)

Tangier's strategic location made it a target for European powers. In 1471, the city was captured by the **Portuguese**, who controlled it for nearly two centuries. In 1661, it was passed to **England** as part of the dowry of Catherine of Braganza when she married King Charles II. The English gave the city its name, "Tangier," and granted it a charter, but faced constant attacks from local Moroccan forces. Finding the cost of defending the garrison too high, the English abandoned Tangier in 1684, destroying the city and its port before leaving.

The city was quickly reclaimed by Moroccan forces under the Alaouite Sultan **Moulay Ismail**. For the next two centuries, it remained under Moroccan rule but became a focal point for European diplomatic and commercial activity.

### Reunification and Modern Era

Tangier was officially reunified with the rest of the country upon Moroccan independence in 1956, and its international status was revoked. For several decades following independence, the city experienced a period of neglect as the central government focused on other urban centers.

However, since the beginning of the 21st century, Tangier has undergone a dramatic renaissance. Under the impetus of King Mohammed VI, the city has benefited from massive investment and development. This includes the construction of **Tanger Med**, one of the largest ports on the Mediterranean, the establishment of new free-trade zones, and a complete overhaul of the city's infrastructure, including a modernized tourism hub at Tanja Marina Bay. Today, Tangier is a vibrant and rapidly growing economic and cultural center, once again leveraging its strategic position to thrive.
