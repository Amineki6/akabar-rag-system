### Overview of Tangier's Cuisine

The provided documents describe the cuisine of Tangier, which is heavily influenced by its location on the Strait of Gibraltar, blending Moroccan staples with Spanish and Mediterranean flavors. Signature local dishes include an abundance of fresh seafood, such as grilled fish and Fish Tagine, and Bissara, a traditional fava bean soup popular in northern Morocco. The city's proximity to Spain is also evident in popular street foods like bocadillos and Moroccan-style tortillas. These are served alongside national staples like Couscous, traditionally eaten on Fridays, and Harira soup.

For dining experiences, the files recommend several key areas. The port and beachfront are the best places for the freshest seafood. The winding streets of the Medina and Kasbah host a mix of small, authentic eateries and more upscale riad restaurants, with some in the Kasbah offering rooftop terraces with sea views. The Ville Nouvelle (new city) is the hub for international cuisine, including Spanish tapas bars and French bistros. A quintessential part of the local culture is visiting one of Tangier's legendary historic cafes, such as Café de Paris or Café Hafa, to enjoy a mint tea.
