### Ancient Origins: Tingis

Tangier is one of North Africa's most ancient cities. According to amazigh  mythology, it was founded by a hero named Sufax, and Greek mythology links its name to the giant Antaeus. Historically, it was established as a Phoenician trading post as early as the 10th century BC. It later became a key Carthaginian settlement before being incorporated into the Roman Empire.

As **Tingis** It became the capital of the Roman province of Mauretania Tingitana. The city was a vital Roman outpost, and remnants of this era can still be found in the region.
