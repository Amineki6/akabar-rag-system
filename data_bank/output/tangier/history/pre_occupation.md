### Islamic Conquest and Dynastic Rule

After the fall of the Roman Empire, Tangier came under the control of the Vandals and then the Byzantine Empire. In the early eighth century, it was conquered by the Umayyad Arab forces led by **Musa bin Nusayr**. The city served as a crucial staging ground for the Tariq ibn Ziyad's invasion and subsequent conquest of the Iberian Peninsula in 711. For the next several centuries, Tangier was ruled by a succession of Berber and Arab dynasties, including the Idrisids, Almoravids, and Almohads.

---

### European Contention (1471-1844)

Tangier's strategic location made it a target for European powers. In 1471, the city was captured by the **Portuguese**, who controlled it for nearly two centuries. In 1661, it was passed to **England** as part of the dowry of Catherine of Braganza when she married King Charles II. The English gave the city its name, "Tangier," and granted it a charter, but faced constant attacks from local Moroccan forces. Finding the cost of defending the garrison too high, the English abandoned Tangier in 1684, destroying the city and its port before leaving.

The city was quickly reclaimed by Moroccan forces under the Alaouite Sultan **Moulay Ismail**. For the next two centuries, it remained under Moroccan rule but became a focal point for European diplomatic and commercial activity.
