Tangier is a city with a rich history and a diverse religious landscape, reflected in its mosques, churches, and synagogues. Here are some of the key religious sites and attractions to visit:

### Mosques

* **Grand Mosque of Tangier:** Located in the city's old medina, this historic mosque has a long and fascinating history. The site has been used for religious purposes since antiquity, originally housing a Roman temple and later a Roman church. It was converted to a mosque by the Marinid dynasty and later rebuilt by Sultan Moulay Slimane in the 19th century. As with most mosques in Morocco, it is generally not open to non-Muslims. However, its beautiful exterior and minaret, decorated with traditional geometric motifs and green tiles, are worth seeing.
* **Kasbah Mosque:** Situated in the Kasbah (the fortified part of the medina), this mosque is another important historical and architectural site.
* **Sidi Bou Abib Mosque:** Built in 1917, this mosque is known for its distinctive Mauresque style and colorful minaret, which is a prominent landmark in the Grand Socco square.

### Churches

* **St. Andrew's Church:** This Anglican church is a charming and historically significant site. Consecrated in 1905, it features a unique blend of architectural styles, including a minaret-inspired bell tower and horseshoe arches. The churchyard is also notable, serving as the final resting place for many prominent figures from Tangier's international past.
* **Roman Catholic Cathedral of Tangier (Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception and the Holy Spirit):** Also known as the Spanish Cathedral, this modernist building was completed in 1961. Its bell tower was once the tallest structure in the city.
* **Church of the Immaculate Conception:** Located in the medina, this Spanish-built church dates back to the late 19th century.
* **French Church of Tangier:** This church is another testament to the city's diverse European influences.

### Synagogues

* **Moshe Nahon Synagogue:** This is one of the last remaining operating synagogues in Tangier and a significant site for understanding the city's once-thriving Jewish community. Located on Rue Synagogue, it is known for its lavish interior with intricate carvings and artifacts.
* **Chaar Rafael Synagogue:** This synagogue, located on Boulevard Pasteur, was converted from a Jewish-owned villa in 1954 and is another surviving remnant of Tangier's Jewish heritage.

### Other Sites of Religious Significance

* **Jewish Cemetery:** This "old cemetery" is a poignant reminder of Tangier's Jewish history, with graves dating back to the 16th century.
* **Tomb of Ibn Battuta:** The tomb of the renowned 14th-century traveler and writer, a native of Tangier, is located in the medina.
