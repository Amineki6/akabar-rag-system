### Where to Eat

#### Kasbah Restaurants
Many of the region's historic kasbahs, both in Ouarzazate and in nearby Aït Benhaddou and Skoura, have been converted into hotels and guesthouses with their own restaurants. Dining within these ancient mudbrick walls is a unique experience, often featuring high-quality traditional Moroccan cuisine served in a beautiful, atmospheric setting.

#### City Center Restaurants
The modern town center of Ouarzazate has a variety of restaurants catering to all tastes and budgets. Here you can find traditional Moroccan eateries, French-inspired bistros, and simple cafes serving classics like tagine and grilled meats.

#### Riads & Guesthouses (Maisons d'Hôtes)
For a truly authentic taste of local cuisine, consider having dinner at a family-run riad or guesthouse. Meals are often prepared by the hosts using fresh, local ingredients and traditional home-cooking methods. These meals are typically available to non-guests with an advance reservation.

#### Local Cafes
For a more casual meal or a quick bite, the local cafes are a great option. They are perfect for enjoying a mint tea, a coffee, or a simple lunch like brochettes or a sandwich.
