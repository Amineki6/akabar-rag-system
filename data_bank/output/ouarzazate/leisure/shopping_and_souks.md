## A Guide to Souks and Shopping in Ouarzazate

Shopping in Ouarzazate offers a chance to discover the rich artistic traditions of the Amazigh people of the Atlas Mountains and the desert. The city is a key center for regional crafts, known for its vibrant carpets, intricate pottery, and handcrafted silver jewelry.

---

### Traditional Souks & Markets

#### Central Market & Medina
Unlike the sprawling, walled souks of other Moroccan cities, the main market area in Ouarzazate is more of a central, open-air souk located in the city center. It's a bustling marketplace where locals shop for daily necessities, fresh produce, spices, and household goods. Within this area and the small medina, you can find numerous shops selling traditional crafts.

#### Souk opposite the Taourirt Kasbah
Directly opposite the main entrance to the historic Taourirt Kasbah, you will find a collection of bazaars and shops specifically catering to visitors. These stores offer a wide variety of souvenirs and regional crafts, making it a convenient stop after exploring the kasbah.

**Key Goods to Look For:**
* **Berber Carpets:** The region is known for its beautiful, handwoven carpets and rugs, often featuring bold geometric patterns and vibrant colors characteristic of local tribes.
* **Pottery:** Look for the distinctive green-glazed pottery from Tamegroute, a village in the nearby Draa Valley.
* **Silver Jewelry:** The area has a rich tradition of Amazigh silver jewelry, often intricate and decorated with semi-precious stones.
* **Saffron:** The nearby town of Taliouine is the heart of Morocco's saffron production, and high-quality saffron can be purchased in the souks.

---

### Artisan Cooperatives

#### Ensemble Artisanal (Artisans' Center)
Located in the city center, the Ensemble Artisanal is a government-sponsored complex that brings together artisans from the region. It's a great place to see craftspeople at work and to purchase high-quality, authentic goods at fixed prices, which eliminates the need for bargaining. You can find a wide range of products, including carpets, leather goods, stonework, and textiles. Visiting the cooperative supports the local artisans directly.
