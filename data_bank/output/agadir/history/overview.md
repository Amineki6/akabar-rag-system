### Overview of Agadir History

These documents provide a comprehensive history of the Moroccan city of Agadir, from its first appearance on a map in 1325 to its current status as a major modern city. The files detail the key periods of its history, including the Portuguese occupation in the early 16th century and their subsequent expulsion by the Sa'di dynasty in 1541.

The narrative covers periods of both prosperity and decline, noting Agadir's importance as a trading hub in the 17th century, its complete destruction by an earthquake in 1731, and a long decline after its port was closed in 1760. The reopening of the port in the late 19th century led to renewed European interest, culminating in the Agadir Crisis of 1911.

The documents also describe the city's development under the French protectorate, its role as a stop for the Aéropostale airmail service, and its rapid growth in the 1950s. A central event covered is the catastrophic earthquake of 1960, which destroyed the old city and led to its complete reconstruction nearby. Finally, the files describe modern Agadir as a vital center for tourism, fishing, and agricultural exports.
