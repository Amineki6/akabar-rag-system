### Overview of Agadir's Cuisine

These documents describe the local cuisine of Agadir and the surrounding Souss region, which is heavily influenced by its location as a major Atlantic port and its Amazigh heritage. The files highlight iconic regional specialties such as Amlou, a spread made from roasted almonds, Argan oil, and honey, and culinary Argan oil, which is endemic to the area.

The local food scene is characterized by an abundance of fresh seafood. Signature dishes include various forms of grilled fish (`Poissons Grillés`), especially sardines, and the local Fish Tagine (`Tagine de Poisson`). Other traditional Amazigh foods mentioned are Tafarnout bread and Tagoula, a barley porridge. National dishes like Harira and Pastilla are also part of the local offerings.

For finding these foods, the documents recommend visiting the Fishing Port, where one can buy the catch of the day and have it grilled on-site. Souk El Had is noted as a major market for trying traditional dishes like white fish tagine and grilled sardines. Other areas mentioned for dining include Talborjt for traditional restaurants and Agadir Bay for more luxurious cafes and restaurants.
