### Museums

* **Musée du Patrimoine Amazigh (Museum of Amazigh Culture)**
    This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Amazigh (Berber) people of the Souss-Massa region. The collection features traditional jewelry, carpets, textiles, tools, and manuscripts, and is particularly known for its extensive collection of Amazigh silver jewelry.

* **Agadir Memorial Museum (Musée Mémorial d'Agadir)**
    Located within the Jardin d'Olhão, this small museum is dedicated to the memory of the devastating 1960 earthquake that destroyed the old city. It features photographs and newspaper clippings from the era, commemorating this pivotal event in Agadir's history.

* **Universe of Argan Museum**
    An experiential museum that tells the story of the endemic Argan tree. It details the traditional methods of producing Argan oil and showcases its importance in local culture and cuisine.

* **Le Centre national du patrimoine rupestre (CNPR)**
    The National Center for Rock Art Heritage (CNPR) in Agadir is an institution established in 1994 with the mission of documenting, studying, and protecting rock art in Morocco.
