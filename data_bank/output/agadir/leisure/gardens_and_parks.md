### Gardens & Parks

* **Jardin d'Olhão (Olhao Garden)**
    A tranquil public garden built to commemorate the twinning agreement between Agadir and Olhão, Portugal. It features distinct Portuguese-style architecture and water features and is also home to the Agadir Memorial Museum.

* **Crocoparc**
    A large zoological and botanical park that is home to over 300 Nile crocodiles. The park also features a rich botanical garden, including a "Blue Garden" with a collection of rare succulents and other exotic plants.

* **Vallée des Oiseaux (Valley of the Birds)**
    A small and shaded public park in the city center that functions as a walk-through aviary. It features a variety of bird species, as well as other animals like wallabies and goats, set amidst a playground and waterfalls, making it a popular green oasis for families.

* **The new Ibn Zaydoun Urban Park
 After having undergone various developments during its existence, notably the integration of a skatepark and a set of sports equipment in the upper part of the garden in 2008, the Ibn Zaydoun Garden today benefits from a total requalification thanks to the Environmental Preservation & Development of green spaces component of the Agadir PDU 2020-2024.
 
* **Souss-Massa National Park
Coastal area with traditional Amazigh village sheltering rare birds, notably the bald ibis


* ** The main football stadium in Agadir is the **Stade Adrar**, also known as the **Grand Stade d'Agadir**.

Here are some key details about it:

* **Capacity:** It has a seating capacity of approximately 45,480. Renovations are planned for the 2030 World Cup, and its capacity may be adjusted.
* **Home Team:** It is the home ground for the local club, Hassania Agadir.
* **Major Events:** The stadium was inaugurated in 2013 and hosted matches for the FIFA Club World Cup that same year. It is also set to be one of the host venues for the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) and the 2030 FIFA World Cup.
* **Location:** It is located to the east of the city of Agadir, at the foot of the Atlas Mountains.

Before the opening of Stade Adrar, the city's main stadium was **Stade Al Inbiâate**, which has a smaller capacity (around 10,000 seats) and served as the former home of Hassania Agadir.
