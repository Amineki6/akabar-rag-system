### Renting a Car

Renting a car in Agadir offers flexibility and freedom to explore the city and surrounding regions like Paradise Valley and Taghazout at your own pace, without being tied to tour schedules or taxi fares.

#### Booking Process
It is highly recommended to book your car online in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons (e.g., July and August), to ensure availability and secure better rates. Rental agencies are located at **Agadir-Al Massira Airport (AGA)** and within the city center. Both major international and local Moroccan rental agencies are available, with local companies often offering competitive rates.

* **Requirements:**
    * A valid driving license from your country of residence.
    * An International Driving Permit (IDP) may be required by some companies. It is essential to verify this requirement before booking.

#### Expected Costs
Costs vary based on car type, rental duration, and season. Manual transmission vehicles are typically more affordable than automatic ones.

* **Economy (e.g., Renault Clio):** €20 - €30 per day
* **Compact SUV:** €35 - €50 per day
* **Luxury:** €80+ per day

#### Insurance Guide
Most rentals include basic third-party liability insurance, but it often comes with a significant excess (deductible). Carefully review the coverage.

* **Common Options:**
    * **Collision Damage Waiver (CDW):** Reduces or eliminates your financial liability for damage to the rental car in the event of an accident.
    * **Personal Accident Insurance (PAI):** Covers medical expenses for the driver and passengers. Check if your personal travel insurance already provides this coverage before purchasing.
* **Pro Tip:** Some credit cards offer rental car insurance as a benefit. Check with your card provider to see if you are covered, which could save you from buying additional insurance.

#### Driving in Agadir
Driving in Agadir is generally manageable, with roads in good condition.

* Drive on the **right side of the road**.
* On roundabouts, give way to traffic that is already circulating.
* Strictly adhere to posted speed limits, as speed cameras are common.
* Stay vigilant for pedestrians, who may cross the road unexpectedly.
