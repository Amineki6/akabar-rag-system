Its urban development has spanned several centuries. The factors and mechanisms that have governed the main stages of its evolution are inseparable from the contexts of its hinterland and the rest of the Kingdom. The urban fabric of Fès and its spatial spread still bear the marks of historical upheavals and regional and national socio-economic transformations.The birth of Fès dates back to the reign of Idriss I, who was looking, in 789, for a location for his capital. The choice of site was made in the valley of the Oued Fès, a small tributary of the Sebou; ravines bring it abundant and rarely depleted spring waters throughout history . The water supply was almost entirely provided by the natural environment through the neighborhoods of the city. As for the advantages of the regional position, they proved to be strategic in that the city needed to maintain its connections throughout its long urban history.The first core of the city of Fes dates back to the 8th century when the left bank of the oued Fès, a tributary of the oued Sebou, was chosen by Idris I to build the capital of his dynasty. His son and successor Idris II consolidated and materialized this choice by expanding this first core to the right bank of the oued Fès. The site offered several advantages (abundance of water resources, availability of construction materials, and strategic position in relation to north-south and east-west communication routes). The arrival of the Merinids marked the expansion of the city of Fes with the establishment of a new extension, Fes Jdid, to the southwest of Fes El Bali, where an administrative center, a royal palace, a commercial space, and surrounding residences were built, enclosed by walls.
