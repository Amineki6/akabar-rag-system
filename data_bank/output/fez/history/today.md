### fez Today

Today, Fez exists as a living testament to its long and storied history, where the past and present coexist in a unique blend of tradition and modernity. The city's current identity is shaped by its role as a UNESCO World Heritage site, its economic reliance on tourism and traditional craftsmanship, and its ongoing efforts to balance preservation with contemporary life.

Preservation and Heritage
*** UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Old Medina (Fes el-Bali) was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1981. This status has been a driving force behind major restoration and preservation projects. International and national organizations are actively involved in restoring historical buildings, including homes, riads, and religious schools, to protect the city's unique architectural heritage.

*** Artisan Revival: Fez's traditional crafts, such as leather tanning, pottery, and metalwork, continue to thrive. While these crafts have faced challenges, they are a central part of the city's economy and cultural identity. Many workshops are still run by families who have passed down skills for generations. The city is a hub for high-quality, authentic Moroccan handicrafts.

Economic and Social Life
*** Tourism: Tourism is the most significant pillar of Fez's modern economy. Visitors from around the world are drawn to the medina's authenticity, historical sites, and vibrant souks. The growth of tourism has led to the transformation of many historic houses into guesthouses (riads) and boutique hotels.

*** The Ville Nouvelle: The modern section of Fez, built during the French Protectorate, serves as the city's administrative and commercial hub. It provides a stark contrast to the medina, with wider streets, modern shops, universities, and commercial services. This division reflects the city's duality, as the modern city supports the daily life of its residents while the old medina remains the cultural heart.

*** Fez's Role in Modern Morocco
Cultural and Intellectual Center: Fez retains its historic role as a center of learning and spirituality. The Al-Qarawiyyin University is still an active institution, and the city continues to attract students and scholars of Islamic and Moroccan studies. The annual Fez Festival of World Sacred Music is an internationally renowned event that reinforces the city's cultural prominence.
