## A Guide to Souks and Shopping in fez

Shopping in Fez is a truly immersive and traditional experience, with the city's medina serving as one of the largest and most authentic marketplaces in the world. The souks of Fes el-Bali are not just for tourists; they are the vibrant commercial heart of the city, where locals buy everything from fresh produce to traditional crafts.

### What to Buy in Fez

Fez is particularly famous for a few key artisan products that make for excellent souvenirs:

* **Leather Goods:** This is the city's most renowned craft. The **Chouara Tannery** is the place to witness the ancient, labor-intensive process of dyeing hides in massive vats. Surrounding the tanneries, shops offer a wide array of leather products, including high-quality bags, wallets, belts, and the iconic Moroccan poufs and *babouches* (leather slippers).
* **Pottery and Ceramics:** Fez is known for its distinctive blue and white pottery, which features intricate geometric patterns. You can find everything from small decorative items to large tagine pots and serving platters. For the most authentic experience, visit the pottery cooperative in **Ain Nokbi**, an artisan quarter outside the medina, where you can see the pottery being made from local gray clay.
* **Metalwork:** Artisans in Fez are masters of metalwork, especially copper and brass. The **Seffarine Square** is the center for coppersmiths, where you can hear the rhythmic hammering of artisans crafting trays, lanterns, teapots, and other decorative items.
* **Textiles and Rugs:** You can find a vast selection of rugs, blankets, and textiles. From intricate, colorful Berber rugs to sophisticated city-style carpets, there is a wide range of styles. If you're buying a rug, remember that bargaining is expected, and it's a good idea to know some of the basics of quality (e.g., smaller knots generally indicate higher quality).
* **Spices and Food:** The souks are a feast for the senses, with stalls piled high with vibrant spices, dried fruits, olives, and mint. **Souk Attarine** is famous for its fragrant spices, herbs, and traditional remedies.

### Popular Souks and Shopping Areas

The souks in Fez are often organized by guild or trade, so you can find specific types of goods in certain areas.

* **Souk al-Attarine:** Known as the "Spice Souk," it's a fragrant and colorful place where you can buy spices, perfumes, and beauty products like henna and argan oil.
* **Seffarine Square:** The "Coppersmiths' Souk" is located here, where you can watch artisans at work and purchase a wide variety of metal goods.
* **Souk Nejjarine:** This souk is dedicated to woodworking. It's located near the Nejjarine Museum and Fountain, and you'll find beautiful carved cedar pieces, from furniture to decorative boxes.
* **Souk Henna:** One of the oldest marketplaces in the medina, this souk sells henna, traditional cosmetics, and ceramics.

### Shopping Tips

* **Bargaining:** Haggling is a fundamental part of the shopping experience in Fez. Start by offering around 50-60% of the asking price and negotiate politely. Don't be afraid to walk away if you can't agree on a price; the vendor may call you back with a better offer.
* **Cash is King:** Most vendors in the medina only accept cash (Moroccan Dirham), so it's essential to have a supply of small bills.
* **Getting Lost is Part of the Fun:** The souks are a maze of winding alleyways, and getting lost is almost inevitable. It’s part of the adventure. You can always ask for directions or hire a guide to help you navigate.
* **Authenticity:** For high-quality, authentic goods, consider shopping at artisan cooperatives or government-certified shops.

### Traditional Souks & Markets

#### Here is a guide to the most traditional souks and what you can find there:

Specialized Souks
*** Souk al-Attarine (The Spice Souk): This is one of the most famous and fragrant souks in the medina. It's a feast for the senses, with mounds of colorful spices, dried fruits, nuts, herbal teas, and traditional remedies piled high. The air is thick with the scent of cumin, saffron, paprika, and other exotic aromas.

*** Souk el-Henna (The Henna Souk): One of the oldest and most pleasant markets, this souk is dedicated to traditional beauty products. You can find henna powder, argan oil, black soap, and other natural cosmetics. It's a great place to learn about the traditional uses of these products.

*** Souk el-Seffarine (The Coppersmiths' Souk): Located in the bustling Seffarine Square, this souk is a noisy and exciting place. You'll hear the constant hammering of artisans shaping copper, brass, and other metals into beautiful lanterns, trays, tea sets, and other household items. It's a true showcase of Moroccan craftsmanship.

*** Souk el-Nejjarine (The Carpenters' Souk): This souk is dedicated to woodworking and is situated near the magnificent Nejjarine Museum and Fountain. Here, you can find a wide variety of items carved from wood, especially the fragrant cedar wood, including furniture, decorative boxes, and musical instruments.

*** Souk el-Jeld (The Leather Souk): Fez is famous for its leather, and this souk is the place to buy it. It's close to the Chouara Tannery, where you can see the hides being dyed. The shops here are filled with an array of leather goods, from traditional Moroccan babouches (slippers) and poufs to jackets and bags.

Other Notable Markets

*** The Food Souks: The medina has multiple food markets where locals shop for daily essentials. These markets are a vibrant spectacle, with vendors selling fresh fruits, vegetables, meat, poultry, olives, and a variety of freshly baked goods. The atmosphere is authentic and lively, offering a true glimpse into daily life in Fez.

*** Kissaria: These are covered markets, often located in the heart of the medina, where you can find more valuable goods like clothing, fabrics, and jewelry. The Kissaria of Fez is particularly known for its fine textiles, including silks, brocades, and traditional caftans.

*** The Tanneries: While not a souk in the traditional sense, the Chouara Tannery is the most famous marketplace in Fez. You can tour one of the shops that overlook the open-air tannery to watch the age-old process of dyeing leather. The experience can be overwhelming due to the strong smells, but it's an essential part of the Fez cultural experience.

### Modern Shopping

While Fez is famous for its ancient souks and traditional craftsmanship, the city also has a modern side, particularly in the Ville Nouvelle (the new city). This area offers a different kind of shopping experience, with fixed prices, air-conditioned stores, and a wider range of international brands and modern goods.

Malls and Shopping Centers

*** Borj Fez Mall: This is the city's largest and most prominent modern shopping center. Located between the old and new parts of the city, it features a mix of international and Moroccan brand-name stores, a large supermarket (Carrefour), a food court with various dining options, a cinema, and entertainment facilities. It’s the go-to place for conventional, fixed-price shopping.

*** The Ville Nouvelle (New City)
The main streets of the Ville Nouvelle offer a more contemporary and relaxed shopping atmosphere than the medina.

*** Avenue Hassan II: This broad, tree-lined boulevard is the main artery of the Ville Nouvelle. Here, you'll find a variety of shops, cafes, and restaurants. While there are some stores selling traditional crafts, the overall style is more European, with boutiques offering clothing, shoes, and home goods.

*** Avenue Mohammed V: This avenue is another central street with modern stores, designer boutiques, and specialty shops. It's a good place to look for electronics, cosmetics, and other modern amenities.

*** Ensemble Artisanal: Located on Avenue Hassan II:
 This is a government-run complex where you can buy high-quality handicrafts at fixed prices. It's a great option for tourists who prefer not to haggle. The items here are often more expensive than in the souks, but you can be sure of the quality and authenticity, and the artists receive a fair price.
