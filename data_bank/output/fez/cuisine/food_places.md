### Where to Eat

Fez is a city with a rich culinary heritage, and dining here is an integral part of the cultural experience. You can find everything from traditional Moroccan feasts in lavish riads to casual street food, offering options for every budget and taste. Fine Dining in Historic Settings Fez is particularly famous for its fine dining restaurants located in beautifully restored dars and riads (traditional Moroccan mansions with courtyards). These offer not just a meal, but an elegant, multi-sensory experience.

#### The Medina
The historic heart of fez is filled with traditional restaurants and small, family-run eateries tucked away in its winding alleys. Dining in the Medina offers an authentic experience, with a focus on classic Moroccan home cooking. It's the best place to find a delicious, traditional tagine or a hearty bowl of Harira.

***Street Food and Local Delights
For an authentic taste of daily life and affordable eats, the street food scene in Fez is a must-try.

Food Stalls near Bab Bou Jeloud: The area just outside the Blue Gate is a bustling hub of activity, especially in the evening. You can find vendors selling everything from roasted meats and skewers to pastries and traditional sweets.


#### Ville Nouvelle (Downtown)
The modern city center, built during the French Protectorate, is home to a wide range of dining options. Here you'll find classic French-style brasseries, established Moroccan restaurants, and numerous cafes perfect for a coffee and a pastry.
