### Popular Neighborhoods for Tourists

#### City Center (Centre Ville)
The bustling heart of the city, the Centre Ville is perfect for first-time visitors and those who want to be close to major attractions like the Old Medina and the Hassan II Mosque. The area is known for its impressive Art Deco architecture and is filled with a wide range of hotels, restaurants, and shops. Staying here puts you in the middle of the action.

#### The Corniche (Ain Diab)
Stretching along the Atlantic coast, the Corniche is Casablanca's seaside entertainment district. This area is ideal for those seeking a resort-like atmosphere with beautiful ocean views. It is lined with beach clubs, upscale restaurants, nightclubs, and large hotels. It's the best choice for a vacation feel with easy access to the beach.

#### Gauthier
A trendy, modern, and relatively central neighborhood, Gauthier is popular with young professionals and travelers looking for a chic, cosmopolitan vibe. It's known for its stylish boutiques, art galleries, and a high concentration of excellent cafes and international restaurants. It offers a sophisticated, urban experience.

#### Maarif
Located just southwest of the city center, Maarif is a vibrant, middle-class neighborhood known for its fantastic shopping opportunities. It's home to the Twin Center (a key city landmark) and is packed with both local and international brand stores. It offers a more local, energetic feel and is a great base for exploring the city.

#### Old Medina
For a more traditional and historic experience, a stay near the Old Medina is a good option. While accommodation within the small, walled medina itself is limited, the surrounding area provides direct access to its bustling souks and historic walls. It offers a glimpse into the city's pre-colonial past, though it is much smaller and less tourist-focused than the medinas of Fes or Marrakech.
