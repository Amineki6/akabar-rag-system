### Leisure & Recreation

* **Stroll Along The Corniche**
    The Corniche is Casablanca's vibrant seaside promenade, stretching along the Atlantic coast in the **Ain Diab** district. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists to walk, jog, and relax. The promenade is lined with a succession of beaches, swimming pools, cafes, restaurants, and beach clubs. It's the perfect place to enjoy the ocean breeze and experience the city's more modern, leisurely side.

* **Shopping Malls**
    Casablanca offers several modern shopping experiences. Located at the end of the Corniche, **Morocco Mall** is one of the largest in Africa, featuring an indoor aquarium and an IMAX cinema. Other popular options for shopping and dining include **Anfaplace Mall** and **Marina Shopping** center.

* **Marché Central (Central Market)**
    For a lively and authentic local experience, visit the Marché Central. Here you can find fresh seafood, flowers, spices, and produce. It's also a great place to enjoy a fresh fish lunch at one of the small, bustling restaurants within the market.

* **Tamaris Aquaparc**
    A popular water park located just outside the city, offering a variety of slides, pools, and water-based activities for a fun family day out.


### Architectural & Cultural Exploration

* **Visit the Hassan II Mosque**
    This is Casablanca's most iconic landmark and an absolute must-see. It is one of the largest mosques in the world, with a minaret that stands as the second tallest. Its stunning location on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean is breathtaking. A significant portion of the mosque's floor is made of glass, allowing worshippers to see the sea below. Unlike most mosques in Morocco, it is open to non-Muslim visitors through guided tours, which showcase the exquisite Moroccan craftsmanship of its interiors.

* **Explore the Art Deco District (Ville Art-Déco)**
    Take a walk through the city center to discover Casablanca's unique architectural heritage. The area around **Mohammed V Square** is filled with buildings from the French Protectorate era, designed in the Mauresque (Moorish revival) and Art Deco styles. Key buildings include the Grande Poste (Main Post Office), the Wilaya (City Hall), and the Palace of Justice.

* **Wander Through the Old Medina**
    While smaller and less tourist-oriented than those in Fes or Marrakech, Casablanca's Old Medina offers an authentic glimpse into the city's past. Navigate its narrow, winding streets to see the historic city walls and explore small, traditional souks selling leather goods, spices, and handmade crafts.

* **Discover the Habous Quarter (New Medina of Casablanca)**
    Built by the French in the 1930s, the Habous Quarter was designed to blend traditional Moroccan architecture with modern urban planning. The result is a charming and orderly district with clean streets, arched walkways, and neat souks. It's an ideal place to shop for high-quality handicrafts, books, and traditional clothing in a more relaxed environment. Don't miss the impressive **Mahkama Du Pacha**, a stunning example of Hispano-Moorish architecture that formerly served as a courthouse.

* **Visit Religious Landmarks**
    Casablanca is home to significant religious sites for different faiths. These include the modern **Church of Notre Dame of Lourdes**, the historical **The Church Of The Sacred Heart**, and the active synagogue, **Temple Beth-El**.

* **See the Royal Palace of Casablanca**
    While the palace is not open to the public, the exterior of this grand royal residence is an impressive sight and showcases opulent Moroccan design.
