### Regional Specialties & Signature Dishes

#### Grilled Seafood & Fried Fish
Thanks to its extensive coastline, Casablanca excels in fresh seafood. The most authentic experience is found at the **Marché Central (Central Market)**, where you can choose from the day's catch—including calamari, shrimp, oysters, and a wide variety of fish—and have it fried or grilled on the spot. It's often served simply with a side of fresh Moroccan salads, bread, and zesty dipping sauces.

#### Tagine
The quintessential Moroccan slow-cooked stew is a staple in Casablanca. While you can find all the classic variations like lamb with prunes or chicken with preserved lemons, the coastal influence makes **Fish Tagine (Tagine de Poisson)** a popular local choice. It typically features firm white fish cooked with tomatoes, potatoes, olives, and a zesty 'chermoula' marinade.

#### Couscous
A national dish, couscous consists of fine, steamed semolina granules served with a rich stew of vegetables and meat (typically lamb, beef, or chicken). Traditionally eaten on Fridays, you'll find it on almost every menu, often as a daily special.

#### Pastilla (B'stilla)
This elaborate and festive pie offers a unique blend of sweet and savory flavors. In Casablanca, alongside the classic pigeon or chicken versions, you can often find a delicious **Seafood Pastilla (Pastilla aux Fruits de Mer)**. This variety is filled with shrimp, calamari, and fish, mixed with vermicelli noodles and a savory, slightly spicy 'chermoula' sauce, all wrapped in flaky 'warqa' pastry.

#### Harira
The traditional Moroccan soup, a hearty and flavorful blend of tomatoes, lentils, chickpeas, and fresh herbs. It's a staple in local eateries and is especially popular in the evenings, often served with dates and a sweet pastry called 'chebakia'.

#### Brochettes (Kebabs)
Grilled meat skewers are a popular and delicious staple. You can find them in both street-side stalls and restaurants, with options ranging from lamb, beef, and chicken to spicy 'merguez' sausage, all grilled over charcoal for a smoky flavor.
