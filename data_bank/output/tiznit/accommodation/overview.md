Tiznit offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and travel styles, from traditional riads within the historic medina to more modern hotels outside the city walls. The lodging here is often characterized by its authentic, local charm.

Here is an overview of the accommodation options in Tiznit:

### 1. Riads (Traditional Guesthouses)
* **Location:** Most riads are located inside the walled medina. Staying in a riad is a highly recommended experience for those who want to immerse themselves in the city's history and culture.
* **Atmosphere:** Riads are traditional Moroccan houses built around a central courtyard. They offer a tranquil and intimate atmosphere, with beautifully decorated rooms and a focus on personalized hospitality. They are perfect for travelers seeking a quiet retreat.
* **Meals:** Many riads offer delicious homemade Moroccan meals, providing an authentic culinary experience.

### 2. Hotels
* **Location:** Hotels in Tiznit are typically found just outside the medina's walls, particularly in the "ville nouvelle" (new city).
* **Types:** You can find a range of hotels, from budget-friendly options to more comfortable, mid-range establishments. They generally offer more standard amenities like air conditioning, and some may have a pool or a restaurant on-site.

### 3. Auberges (Hostels and Inns)
* **Location:** Scattered throughout the city and its surroundings.
* **Purpose:** Auberges provide a more basic and budget-friendly alternative. They are popular with backpackers, cyclists, and travelers on a tight budget. They often serve as a convenient and no-frills place to rest.

### 4. Camping
* **Outdoor Experience:** For those traveling with a tent or camper van, Tiznit has a well-known municipal campsite. It's a popular choice for overlanders and offers basic facilities like showers and toilets. It also provides a unique opportunity to interact with other travelers from around the world.

### Tips for Booking in Tiznit
* **Book in Advance:** While Tiznit is not as crowded as major tourist hubs, it's a good idea to book in advance, especially during the high season or festival periods.
* **Cash:** While some larger hotels may accept credit cards, many riads and smaller establishments operate on a cash-only basis.
* **Explore Both Parts of the City:** Staying in the medina offers a historical experience, while staying in the new city offers more convenience and access to modern amenities.
