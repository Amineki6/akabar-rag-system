** The Small Museum of Sahrawi Morocco: 
built in the city of Dakhla, plays the largest role in defining Sahrawi culture and Hassani society, and celebrates the tangible and intangible heritage and the specificity that characterizes Sahrawi society in Morocco.


 ** The Dakhla-Oued Ed-Dahab region, like other parts of Morocco, has a **"Space of Historical Memory of the Resistance and Liberation"**. These centers are part of a national network established by Morocco's High Commissioner for Former Resistance Fighters and Former Members of the Liberation Army.

### Purpose and Role

The main objective of this space is to preserve the historical memory of Moroccan resistance against colonialism and the Liberation Army's efforts to complete the country's territorial integrity. Specifically, in Dakhla, it focuses on the struggles in the southern provinces against Spanish occupation and the events leading to the region's return to Moroccan sovereignty in 1979.

The Dakhla center plays a vital role in:

* **Documenting History:** It collects and archives documents, photos, and oral testimonies from former resistance fighters and members of the Liberation Army.
* **Educational Activities:** It organizes educational and cultural programs, including exhibitions, conferences, and workshops, to teach younger generations about the country's history and national values.
* **Commemorating Events:** The space is central to commemorating key historical events, such as the anniversary of the Green March and the return of the Oued Ed-Dahab province to Morocco.

These spaces serve as a connection between the past and present, ensuring that the legacy of those who fought for independence and national unity is remembered and honored.
