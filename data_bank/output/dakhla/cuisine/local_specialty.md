The Sahraoui Village is a privileged place to discover Sahraoui gastronomy. One can savor iconic dishes such as "maro au poisson" (also called Tieb), a rice dish with fresh fish that is highly appreciated in the region. These meals reflect the nomadic history of the Sahraoui, with simple yet rich flavors, often prepared with local ingredients, honoring ancestral traditions.


The  Sahrawi Moroccan Tea RitualTea holds a central place in Sahrawi culture. Served in three successive rounds (bitter, sweet, then very sweet), it is much more than a drink: it is a social ritual that marks the exchanges and moments of conviviality. This ritual takes on its full meaning in the desert context, between sand and sea, and symbolizes the hospitality that is so cherished by the Sahrawis.

Camel milk holds a prestigious status in the Moroccan desert, as it is served throughout the day and is preferred at night. Goat milk tops the list of beverages.


Dishes from the desert cuisine are widely known for their luxury and diversity, highlighted by their wonderful and distinctive flavors that are based on natural and beneficial ingredients. The people of the desert rely on camel and goat meat in their meals, which adds a rich character to the dishes.
