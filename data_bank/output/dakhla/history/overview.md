The history of Dakhla is relatively modern, shaped by its strategic location and its role during the colonial era. While the region has been inhabited for centuries by nomadic Sahrawi tribes, the city as it is known today was officially established in the late 19th century. Its history can be divided into three main periods: pre-colonial, the Spanish colonial era, and its modern history under Moroccan sovereignty.

Modern Development: Since 1979, Dakhla has undergone significant development. It has transformed from a small administrative town into a major economic hub, a thriving port, and an international tourist destination, especially for water sports. The Moroccan government has invested heavily in infrastructure and development projects to integrate the city fully into the national economy and promote its potential.
