The mosques of Taroudant

* **The Great Mosque of Taroudant, named Al Aâdham:
Renovated by Muhammed ach-Chaykh of the Saadian dynasty around the middle of the 16th century, this mosque is one of the most important and largest of the Saadian era.Located to the east of the city, this mosque currently covers an area of 3,215 square meters and can accommodate up to 4,000 worshippers. It is distinguished by its 27-meter tall minaret. This square minaret is one of the most famous in Morocco. The mosque is also characterized by a richly decorated mihrab adorned with kufic letters. A place of worship, but also of teaching, the Great Mosque includes a library, once endowed with an important collection of manuscripts and precious documents that have been patiently stored over the centuries.

* **The Kasbah Mosque:
 is considered one of the prominent historical landmarks in the city, and it was greatly affected by the earthquake that struck the region in September 2023. Since then, restoration and repair works have remained pending, raising concerns among residents who view the mosque as an important part of their religious and cultural heritage.


* **The Mosque of Farq al-Ahbab:
 is located in the city center in the neighborhood of (Farq al-Ahbab), with an area of about 1460 square meters. It was founded by Sultan Abdullah al-Ghalib bin Muhammad al-Sheikh al-Saadi in the second half of the 10th century AH / 16th century AD and was named the new mosque, with shops nearby and gardens outside the city walls.One of its unique features is that its minaret was built above its southern entrance. Meanwhile, its western door, opposite the mosque’s ablution area, was small and was known as the Door of (Al-Hafa), referring to the removal of shoes by those entering the mosque courtyard after washing and cleaning their feet from the nearby fountain, and this name was also used for the large arched door that has now vanished.

### Koranic Schools and Islamic Education

Traditional education has always been an essential component of the cultural and religious life of Taroudant. Since the 16th century, the city has been a center for Islamic learning, as evidenced by the **Madrasa of the Great Mosque**, a place for the study of religious jurisprudence and sciences.

In the modern era, this tradition continued with the founding of the **Islamic Institute in 1959** by King Mohammed V. This institute was created to preserve and promote Islamic education, playing a major role in the training of religious scholars and teachers in the region.



* **Jewish Religious and Historical Sites

* **Jewish Cemetery: Taroudant had a significant Jewish community that played an important role in the city's caravan trade. While the community has largely emigrated, there are still remnants of their history. The Jewish cemetery in Taroudant, and another one in the nearby Tiout Oasis, is a place to reflect on this history.

* ** Tombs of Jewish Saints: The region around Taroudant holds the tombs of several Jewish saints, which are considered pilgrimage sites. These include the tomb of Rabbi Shalom Benzaquin in Tiout and the tomb of Rabbi Daoud Ben Baroukh in Souk Tlat de Mnahba, on the road to the Tizi n'Test pass.

* ** Former Synagogue: Taroudant's old synagogue has reportedly been transformed into an antique shop. This gives a glimpse into the city's past and the coexistence of different communities.
