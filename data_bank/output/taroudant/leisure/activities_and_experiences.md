Taroudant is an exceptional destination blending breathtaking natural landscapes, Berber cultural riches, and numerous outdoor activities for all tastes.

 Taroudant offers a variety of activities and leisure options for visitors, ranging from mountain excursions to strolls through palm groves.
* ** Here are some must-not-miss activities in Taroudant: Visit the ramparts: The ramparts of Taroudant, built in the 16th century, are architectural masterpieces that surround the city and provide panoramic views of the region.
* ** Markets and souks: 

The souks of Taroudant, such as the Arab souk and the Berber souk, are places for buying and selling local products, where visitors can discover the richness of local craftsmanship.

* ** Nature excursions: Excursions are organized to explore the surrounding landscapes, including lush valleys and amazigh villages.

* ** Traditional tanneries: Visit the tanneries to appreciate the local artisan skills and discover the materials used for tanning.

* ** Palm groves: The palm grove of Taroudant is an ideal place for a walk or bike ride, where visitors can encounter farmers and enjoy the greenery.These activities allow visitors to fully enjoy Moroccan culture, the surrounding nature, and local craftsmanship. 

* **Taroudant is a destination that combines history, gastronomy, and magnificent landscapes, offering a unique experience to those who venture to visit the Atlas Mountains .

* **Explore the Atlas Mountains on a hike A few kilometers from Taroudant, the first foothills of the Anti-Atlas offer spectacular landscapes: green valleys, Berber villages, orange orchards, natural springs. Excursions are organized for half a day or a full day, with or without a guide, to discover this region still preserved from mass tourism.

* **Admire the ramparts of Taroudant at sunset. The earthen ramparts of Taroudant, extending over 7 km, surround the medina and are among the best-preserved in Morocco. The golden light of the setting sun enhances them, offering a majestic sight not to be missed. A carriage ride around the ramparts is a unique way to discover them.

Events and Festivities

* ** International Taroudant Short Film Festival-This festival is a national competition of short films made by Moroccans, either amateurs or professionals, who have already won a grand prize at a film event held in Morocco.
* ** Saffron Festival of TaliouineCreated in 2007, this festival immerses us in the aromas of saffron with visits to saffron fields and cooperatives, a forum for associations on fair trade, demonstrations of the use of the spice, workshops, and of course tastings of saffron-based dishes prepared by village women. Sports events are also organized (marathon and football tournament). The saffron festival aims to promote the Taliouine region (in the Taroudant province, a little over 200 km east of Agadir) and to encourage the marketing of saffron to improve the living conditions of the rural populations of the Anti-Atlas.

* ** The 'Dakka and Rhythm of Taroudant' festival takes place at the end of June. It provides an opportunity to see different folkloric and artistic groups perform from various regions of Morocco. The two days of the festival aim to showcase the art of dakka and the rhythms of Ahwach, Ahdidous, and Gnaoua. It will also be a chance to discover groups from the Sahara and Africa, as well as those from the youth of Taroudant.
