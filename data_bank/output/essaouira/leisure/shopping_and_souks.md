Essaouira's medina is a fantastic place for shopping, offering a more relaxed experience than the bustling souks of Marrakech or Fes. The city is renowned for specific local crafts and products, making for unique souvenirs and gifts.

### What to Buy in Essaouira

Essaouira's distinct atmosphere and historical trades mean you'll find a variety of items here that are either unique to the region or particularly well-crafted.

* **Thuya Wood Crafts**: This is the city's most famous and celebrated handicraft. Artisans use the fragrant, reddish-brown thuya wood to create a wide range of products, from intricate inlaid boxes and chess sets to decorative tables, bowls, and even furniture. The art of marquetry (inlaying different materials like mother-of-pearl or other woods) is a specialty here.
* **Argan Oil**: Essaouira is located in the heart of the Argan-producing region. You can buy high-quality, authentic Argan oil for both culinary and cosmetic use. Look for products from women's cooperatives, which ensure fair trade and support local communities.
* **Jewelry**: Historically, Essaouira had a large Jewish community that were expert jewelers. Today, you can still find beautiful silver and gold pieces, often with Berber-inspired designs and semi-precious stones. The **Souk des Bijoutiers** (Jewelry Souk) is the place to go for this.
* **Leather Goods**: Essaouira's leatherwork is known for its quality. You can find a variety of bags, shoes, and belts in the souks. The city is especially known for its unique **raffia shoes and bags**.
* **Textiles and Carpets**: While Essaouira is not as famous as other cities for its rugs, you can still find a selection of high-quality Moroccan carpets and blankets.
* **Spices and Foodstuffs**: The local souks are filled with vibrant spices, olives, honey, and **Amlou** (a delicious spread made from roasted almonds, Argan oil, and honey).

### Where to Shop

* **The Medina's Main Streets**: The main arteries of the medina, like Avenue de l'Istiqlal, are lined with shops selling all kinds of goods. It's easy to wander and discover treasures.
* **The Souks**: Unlike the vast, labyrinthine souks of Marrakech, Essaouira's markets are more manageable. They are often clustered by product type, making it easier to navigate.
    * **Souk des Bijoutiers**: The covered jewelry souk.
    * **Souk des Grains**: A spice and food market.
    * **Souk Jdid**: A general market selling a variety of goods.
* **Artisan Cooperatives**: For high-quality, ethically sourced products like Argan oil and thuya wood, consider visiting a local cooperative. These places offer fixed prices and a chance to see the artisans at work.
* **Art Galleries**: Essaouira has a thriving art scene. Many artists, inspired by the city's light and atmosphere, have opened galleries in the medina where you can buy paintings and sculptures.

### Tips for Shopping

* **Bargaining**: While bargaining is a part of the shopping experience in Morocco, It's still good practice to negotiate, but you might find some shops with fixed prices.
* **Authenticity**: Be sure to check the quality of what you're buying, especially for items like Argan oil, which is sometimes diluted. Buying from a cooperative is a good way to ensure authenticity.
* **Shipping**: If you buy large items like carpets or furniture, many shop owners can arrange for international shipping.
