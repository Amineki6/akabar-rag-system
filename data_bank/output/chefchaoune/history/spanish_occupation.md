Chefchaouen's peaceful, isolated nature changed dramatically with the arrival of the Spanish. Its occupation as part of the Spanish Protectorate of northern Morocco in the 20th century marked a new and transformative period in its history.

### The Arrival of the Spanish

After remaining largely closed to outsiders for centuries, Chefchaouen was occupied by Spanish forces in **1920**. The city became a key part of the Spanish Protectorate, which was established in 1912. The Spanish saw Chefchaouen as a strategic and symbolically important city due to its history and its location in the heart of the rebellious Rif region.

### The Rif War and a Brief Independence

Spanish control was not without fierce resistance. Chefchaouen became a central point in the **Rif War (1921-1926)**, led by the leader **Abd el-Krim**. His forces successfully pushed the Spanish out of Chefchaouen in **1924**. This period of independence for the city, as part of the short-lived Republic of the Rif, lasted until the Spanish, with the aid of the French, retook it in **1926**.

### Lasting Impacts of the Occupation

The Spanish occupation, which lasted until Morocco's independence in **1956**, left a visible and lasting legacy on Chefchaouen:

* **Architecture:** The Spanish were responsible for building much of the "new town" outside the medina. They also introduced a Spanish architectural style to some parts of the medina, including the construction of the famous **Spanish Mosque** on the hill overlooking the city.
* **Language:** Due to the long period of Spanish rule, many locals in Chefchaouen speak Spanish fluently, making it a common second language after Moroccan Arabic. This is in contrast to the French-speaking population of cities in the former French Protectorate.
* **Opening to the World:** The Spanish presence brought an end to Chefchaouen's centuries of isolation. It began to be mapped and documented, and its unique charm was slowly introduced to the outside world, setting the stage for its future as a global tourist destination.
