Chefchaouen is a small city, but it is equipped with a public hospital and a network of private clinics and pharmacies to serve its residents and visitors. While the medical facilities are generally adequate for basic care, for major emergencies or complex treatments, it is often recommended to seek care in larger cities like Tetouan or Tangier.

### Public Hospital

* **Hospital Mohammed V (Hôpital Mohammed V):** This is the main public hospital in Chefchaouen. It provides general medical services, emergency care, and some specialized treatments. The hospital is located on the outskirts of the medina, and it is the primary facility for more serious medical issues.

### Private Clinics and Pharmacies

* **Private Clinics:** The city has several private clinics (**Clinique Privée**) that offer a range of services, including general consultations, basic diagnostics, and minor procedures. These clinics are often preferred by tourists for their efficiency and quality of service, though they are more expensive than public facilities.
* **Pharmacies (Pharmacie):** Pharmacies are widely available throughout the city, particularly in the new town and around the main streets. They are easily identifiable by the green crescent sign. Pharmacists in Morocco are well-trained and can provide advice and over-the-counter medications for common ailments like colds, fevers, and minor digestive issues.

### General Advice for Travelers

* **Travel Insurance:** It is highly recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and potential medical evacuation.
* **Minor Issues:** For minor health concerns, such as a cold or a stomach ache, visiting a local pharmacy is often the most convenient and effective solution. The pharmacists can be very helpful.
* **Emergency Services:** In case of a medical emergency, you can ask your hotel or riad staff for assistance. They can help you contact emergency services or arrange transportation to the nearest hospital or clinic.
* **Payment:** Public hospitals may charge a fee for services, while private clinics and pharmacies require payment upfront. It is important to carry some cash with you.
