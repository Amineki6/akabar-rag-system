The culinary scene in Chefchaouen is a delightful mix of traditional Moroccan flavors and relaxed, casual eateries. Dining here is a pleasure, with most places offering beautiful rooftop terraces and a cozy, authentic atmosphere.

### Traditional Moroccan Cuisine

Many establishments serve classic Moroccan dishes, often with a regional Rif twist. You'll find several restaurants known for their authentic and delicious cuisine.

* Some places are particularly recommended for their **tajines** and **couscous**, the latter being a staple on Fridays.
* There are fine-dining options with stunning views, as well as older, more intimate establishments that are perfect for a cozy meal by the fireplace.
* You can also find dining spots located within old riads, offering a traditional ambiance and a great setting to enjoy local cuisine.

### Cafes and Casual Dining

For a simpler meal, a quick snack, or just a place to relax with a cup of mint tea, the city is full of charming cafes.

* A well-known social and culinary hub offers a diverse menu, blending local traditions with more modern flavors. It's often a lively place that hosts cultural events.
* You'll find small cafes that are favorites among both locals and visitors, known for their friendly service, generous portions, and affordable prices. Some of these are great for vegetarian options.
* On the main square, there are plenty of cafes perfect for people-watching, serving a variety of Moroccan and international dishes, as well as delicious breakfasts.

### Other Notable Food Places

* If you want a break from Moroccan food, you can find restaurants serving international dishes, such as pizza.
* Local establishments are highly regarded for desserts, offering delicious ice cream and pastries.
* Don't miss the chance to try **street food**, especially for breakfast. Look for stalls selling hot **bissara** (a thick fava bean soup), freshly made bread, and other local specialties.
