Shopping in Chefchaouen is a delightful and more relaxed experience. The city's main shopping area is entirely contained within the enchanting blue medina.

### What to Buy in Chefchaouen

The city is particularly known for its high-quality, regionally specific handicrafts. Here are some of the most popular items to look for:

* **Wool Products:** Due to the region's climate and history, Chefchaouen is famous for its woolen goods. You can find high-quality wool blankets, **Jellabas** (traditional hooded robes), and the unique **Yebliya hats** (straw hats adorned with colored wool pompoms).
* **Leather Goods:** Local artisans craft beautiful leather items, including babouches (soft leather slippers), bags, wallets, and belts.
* **Handwoven Baskets:** These are a prominent local craft, made from dwarf palm. You'll find a wide variety of baskets, hats, and bags with intricate designs.
* **Local Cosmetics and Herbal Products:** Chefchaouen has a reputation for its organic and natural goods. Look for handmade soaps, argan oil, and a variety of herbs and spices at small apothecaries within the medina.
* **Rifian Ceramics:** The pottery from the Rif region is distinctive, often featuring light clay with black patterns that vary depending on the village of origin.
* **Artwork:** Many shops sell paintings and other artworks that capture the essence of the city's blue streets and iconic architecture.

### Where to Shop

* **The Medina:** The entire old city is a pedestrian-friendly shopping zone. The best way to shop is simply to wander the winding, blue streets and discover small, hidden workshops and boutiques.
* **Plaza Uta el-Hammam:** This central square is a major hub of commercial activity. It's surrounded by shops, and many vendors set up stalls here, especially in the afternoon.
* **Ensemble Artisanat:** Located near Plaza el Majzen, this is a small cooperative workshop. It's a great place to see artisans at work and purchase high-quality goods at fixed prices, so you don't have to haggle.
* **Individual Shops:** Don't be afraid to venture into the smaller shops. Many artisans sell their own goods directly, and you can often find unique items and hear the stories behind their crafts.

### Shopping Tips

* **Bargaining:** Haggling is a customary part of the shopping experience in Morocco. It's expected, but always be respectful and friendly. It’s often a friendly negotiation rather than an aggressive confrontation.
* **Cash is King:** While some larger shops may accept credit cards, it's always best to carry cash (Moroccan Dirhams) for smaller purchases and at the markets.
* **Take Your Time:** The leisurely atmosphere of Chefchaouen means you can take your time browsing without feeling rushed or pressured. Many shopkeepers are happy to offer you a glass of mint tea as you browse.
