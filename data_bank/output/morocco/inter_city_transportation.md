# Transportation in Morocco

## Bus Travel

### Major Companies
* Morocco has local bus companies as well as larger companies like Supratours and CTM.
* **Supratours:**
    * Owned by the train company ONCF.
    * Timetables are available on Supratours.ma.
    * Tickets can be bought online via the ONCF website (oncf.ma) or at the station.
    * Traveling with large luggage requires a separate sticker that costs around 5 dirhams.
* **CTM:**
    * Timetables are available on Ctm.ma.
    * Tickets can be purchased online.

### Bus Station Locations
* **Marrakech:**
    * The Supratours bus station is located next to the train station.
    * The CTM station is a 10-minute walk from the Supratours station.
    * The local bus station is at the Bab Doukkala gate.
    * A taxi from the Supratours/train station to the old town with a meter costs around 30 DH.
* **Essaouira:**
    * The Supratours station is located just outside the Medina walls.
    * The CTM station is farther from the Medina than the Supratours station.
* **Agadir:**
    * All bus companies depart from one main station.
    * The Supratours and CTM ticket windows are at the bottom of the station, near the entrance to the bus parking area.

### Sample Fare
* A bus ticket from Agadir to Marrakech costs around 110 DH (€10).

## Train Travel

### General Information
* The national train company is ONCF, with timetables available on Oncf.ma where tickets can also be purchased online.
* Train tickets are sold in 1st and 2nd class. A 2nd class ticket does not guarantee a seat.
* There are no train lines to the cities of Agadir or Essaouira.

### Special Services
* **Night Train:** A night train runs from Marrakech to Tangier. Sleeper tickets for this train can only be purchased at the train station, not online.
* **High-Speed Train (Al Boraq):** A high-speed train runs between Casablanca and Tangier. It covers the 330 km distance in 2 hours and costs 220 DH (€22).

* **Marrakech ↔ Fez:** 7 hours
* **Marrakech ↔ Meknes:** 6 hours

* **Tangier ↔ Marrakech (night train):** 11 hours
* **Tangier ↔ Fez:** 5 hours

## Local & Urban Transport

### Airport Transfers
* At the exit of any airport, you can take shuttle buses, taxis, or call a car rental agency.

### Tramway
* The tramway is a preferred means of transport in the cities of Rabat, Salé, and Casablanca.
* Two lines connect Salé to Rabat.
* In Casablanca, two additional lines will soon be added to the two existing ones.

### Taxis
* Taking a cab is very common for travel within a city.
* Taxis are identified by color, which varies by city:
    * **Red:** Casablanca and Agadir.
    * **Blue:** Rabat, Tangier, and Asilah.
    * **Orange:** Marrakech.
* For late-night arrivals or important trips, you can book a cab ahead using the "on-demand taxi" or "green taxi" service.

### Tourist Transport
* **Sightseeing Bus:** This is an option in Marrakech and Tangier. Tickets can be bought online for City Tour Tanger and City Tour Marrakech.
* **Small Tourist Train:** In Agadir and Ifrane, small tourist trains are used for pleasant strolls to explore the cities.
