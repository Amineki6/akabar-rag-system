### **Customs Information for Visitors**This guide is provided by the Kingdom of Morocco's Customs and Excise Administration.

#### **Upon Arrival in Morocco**Upon arrival at a port, airport, or land border, you will find two customs lanes.
***Green Lane:** For travelers with nothing to declare.
***Red Lane:** For travelers who have goods to declare.

**1. Currencies and Means of Payment**
*The import of foreign banknotes and bearer negotiable instruments is free but must be declared if the value is equal to or greater than 100,000 Moroccan Dirhams (MAD).
*This declaration must be made upon arrival during customs control and no later declaration will be accepted.
*The declaration is valid for one stay for a period of up to 6 months and must be kept to justify the origin of the currency upon departure.
*Declaring amounts less than 100,000 MAD is optional but can be useful to justify the funds upon exit.

**2. Personal Items (Duty-Free Allowance)**Travelers can import the following personal items without customs formalities or payment of duties:
*Personal effects currently in use.
***Perfume and Toilet Water:** One bottle of perfume (150 ml) and one bottle of cologne (250 ml).
***Tobacco:** 200 grams of manufactured tobacco.
***Alcoholic Drinks:** One 1-liter bottle of wine and one 1-liter bottle of spirits or other alcohol.
***Gifts & Non-Commercial Objects:** Objects in limited quantity with no commercial nature are allowed up to a total value of 2,000 MAD. Any goods exceeding this value are subject to duties and taxes.

**3. Medications**
*Medications for personal use are admitted duty-free and do not require prior authorization from the Ministry of Health.
*You must present supporting documents (e.g., medical certificate, prescription) and commit to using them for personal use only and re-exporting any unused amount.

**4. Special Categories of Goods**
***Companion Animals:** Pets must be accompanied by health documents issued by the official health authorities of the exporting country no more than 3 days before boarding.They are subject to a veterinary health control upon arrival in Morocco.
***Food Products:** Processed food products for personal consumption are generally exempt from control[cite: 68].However, fresh or natural products of animal origin are subject to control.
***Plant Products:** Require a phytosanitary certificate from the ONSSA (National Office of Sanitary Safety of Food products).
***Hunting Weapons:** Importation requires an authorization from the National Security (Police) departments.
***Endangered Species (Fauna and Flora):** Require a CITES certificate issued by the department in charge of Waters and Forests.
***Drones (Unmanned Flying Machines):** Importation of drones by private individuals under the temporary admission regime is strictly prohibited.Failure to declare them will result in confiscation and a penalty.

**5. Prohibited Goods**The importation of the following is prohibited:
*Weapons and ammunition.
*Narcotics.
*All writings, printed materials, and objects that are contrary to good morals and public order.

**6. Motor Vehicles**
*Vehicles can be temporarily imported for a maximum of six months.
***Eligible Vehicles:** Private passenger cars (including rentals), vans, pickup trucks for tourist stays, camper-vans, and scooters/motorcycles with an engine over 50 cm³.
* **Required Documents:**
    *Original vehicle registration certificate.
    *Passport.
    *A legalized power of attorney if the vehicle belongs to someone else.
    *The rental agreement if the vehicle is rented.
***Urgent Return:** If you must leave Morocco without your vehicle, you can make arrangements with customs to store the vehicle and sign a commitment to regularize its situation later.

**7. Pleasure Boats**
*Can be temporarily imported for a maximum of six months[cite: 118].This period can be extended to eighteen months if the boat stays in a marina.
*Within 24 hours of arrival, you must go to the customs office to get a temporary admission document for the vessel.
***Required Documents:** Nationality certificate, title deed, registration certificate, seaworthiness certificate, passenger list, ship's stores list, and a power of attorney or rental agreement if not the owner.

#### **Upon Departure from Morocco**

**1. Currencies and Means of Payment**
*You must declare and justify the origin of currencies valued at 100,000 MAD or more to the exit customs office.
*Proof can include the currency declaration form from your arrival, bank debit notices, or exchange slips.

**2. Cultural Goods**
*The exportation of art, antiques, and collectibles requires an authorization from the department in charge of cultural affairs.

**3. Tax Refund (VAT)**
*Visitors can make purchases and get a refund on the Value Added Tax (VAT).
* **Conditions:**
    *The purchase must be a retail sale of a tourist nature.
    *The purchase amount must be 2,000 MAD or more (including VAT), made on the same day from the same seller.
    *The goods must be exported by the end of the third month following the purchase date.
***Exclusions:** The tax refund does not apply to food, tobacco, medications, unmounted gemstones, and weapons, among other things[cite: 149].
***Process:** The seller provides an export sales slip, which must be stamped by customs upon your departure.The refund is processed by authorized companies ("Global Blue Morocco" and "Morocco Tourist Refund SA").
