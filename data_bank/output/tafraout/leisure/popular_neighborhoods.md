Tafraout is a small town where leisure activities are deeply tied to the surrounding landscape and the traditional villages that make it up. It doesn't have distinct urban neighborhoods; the "popular neighborhoods" are, in fact, the villages and valleys surrounding the main town that offer the main attractions.

Here are the popular "neighborhoods" or areas of Tafraout to explore:

### 1. The Tafraout Town Center
This is the heart of the town, where you'll find most amenities:
* The **weekly souk** (Wednesday market).
* **Hotels and guesthouses**, some offering panoramic mountain views.
* **Cafes and restaurants** where locals and visitors gather.
* It serves as the ideal starting point for hikes and excursions to the surrounding sites.

---

### 2. The Ammeln Valley
This is the most iconic and visited area of Tafraout. Nestled at the foot of Jbel Lkest, this valley is a true gem:
* **Traditional Villages:** It's a collection of over twenty picturesque small Berber villages (douars), with colorful houses that blend into the landscape.
* **The Traditional House Museum in Oumesnat:** A must-see to discover local architecture and traditions.
* **Hiking:** It's a hiker's paradise, with trails that wind through almond groves and palm plantations.

---

### 3. The Aït Mansour Gorges
Located southeast of Tafraout, these gorges are a spectacular site:
* **Unique Scenery:** The valley is lush with a thriving palm grove, offering a striking contrast to the arid rocks.
* **Activities:** Ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and enjoying the natural beauty of the oasis.

---

### 4. The Painted Rocks Area
Located at Aguerd Oudad, a few kilometers from Tafraout, this area is a unique attraction:
* **Open-Air Art:** This is a place where Belgian artist Jean Vérame painted giant boulders, creating a surreal and colorful work of art in the middle of the desert.
* **Easy Hiking:** The site is easily accessible on foot or by bike from the town center.

---

### 5. The Fortified Villages (Agadirs)
Several fortified villages, like the **Tizourgane Kasbah** and the **Agadir Tasguent**, are a short distance from Tafraout. They are remarkable examples of Berber defensive architecture and offer breathtaking views of the region.

In short, while Tafraout doesn't have urban neighborhoods, its "popular neighborhoods" are the natural areas and villages that surround it. Exploring them is the best way to discover the essence of the town and its culture.
