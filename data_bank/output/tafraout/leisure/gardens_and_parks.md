Tafraout is a small town known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities, rather than for formal, manicured gardens and parks typical of larger cities. The entire landscape around Tafraout can be considered its "park," with a focus on hiking, climbing, and exploring the natural environment.

Instead of dedicated public parks with lawns and benches, the "gardens and parks" of Tafraout are the natural wonders of the region:

* **The Ammeln Valley:** This is perhaps the closest thing to a "garden" in the traditional sense. It's a lush, green valley with terraced fields, almond and olive groves, and traditional Berber villages. You can walk along old paths and enjoy the tranquil scenery.

* **The Painted Rocks:** This is a unique "art park" in the desert. The landscape itself becomes a canvas with giant granite boulders painted in bright colors by artist Jean Vérame. It's an interesting place to walk around and take photos.

* **The Palm Groves:** Tafraout is surrounded by extensive palm groves that provide a cool, shady environment for walks, especially in the afternoon.

* **The Gorges of Aït Mansour:** This is a stunning natural area with a river, palm trees, and impressive rock formations. It's a great place for a hike or a day trip to experience a natural oasis.

In essence, the leisure activities in Tafraout are centered on the vast, rugged, and beautiful natural landscape. The focus is on exploring the outdoors and experiencing the unique geology and culture of the Anti-Atlas mountains.
