### Overview of Marrakech's Attractions

The provided documents describe Marrakech as a city rich with historical sites, vibrant markets, and diverse activities, centered around its ancient Medina. The city's heart is the Djemaa el-Fna square, which transforms at night into an open-air spectacle of food stalls, musicians, and storytellers. Key historical landmarks include the iconic Koutoubia Mosque, the opulent Bahia Palace, the atmospheric ruins of El Badi Palace, the rediscovered Saadian Tombs, and the architecturally stunning Ben Youssef Madrasa. The city also boasts numerous museums, such as the Yves Saint Laurent Museum, the House of Photography, and the Boucharouite Museum, which is dedicated to traditional Berber carpets.

Shopping is a core part of the Marrakech experience, focused on the labyrinthine souks of the Medina. These markets are a collection of specialized areas, such as Souk el-Attarine for spices and Souk Semmarine for leather goods and textiles. For a modern, fixed-price shopping experience, the new city district of Gueliz offers international brands and contemporary boutiques. Key neighborhoods for tourists include the historic Medina, the modern Gueliz, the upscale Hivernage district, and the tranquil Palmeraie, a vast palm grove with luxury resorts.

For leisure and entertainment, visitors can escape to serene gardens like the famous Jardin Majorelle, Le Jardin Secret, and the historic Menara Gardens. Adventure activities include sunrise hot air balloon rides, quad biking in the Palmeraie or Agafay Desert, and classic camel rides. The city's nightlife is varied, ranging from rooftop bars overlooking the Medina to sophisticated nightclubs in Hivernage and traditional dinner shows featuring Moroccan cuisine and entertainment. Popular day trips include excursions to the nearby Atlas Mountains and the rocky Agafay Desert.
