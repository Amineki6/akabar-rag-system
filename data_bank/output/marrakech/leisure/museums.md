### Museums

* **Yves Saint Laurent Museum (YSL)**
    A tribute to the iconic fashion designer who drew so much inspiration from Marrakech. The museum showcases a vast collection of his haute couture creations, sketches, and accessories. The building itself is a modern architectural marvel.

* **House of Photography (Maison de la Photographie)**
    Located in a beautifully restored riad, this museum houses a private collection of over 8,000 photographs of Morocco, dating from 1870 to 1950. It offers a fascinating visual journey into the country's past and diverse cultures. The rooftop café provides one of the best panoramic views of the Atlas Mountains and the surrounding Medina.

* **The Orientalist Museum (Musée de l'Art Orientaliste)**
    This private museum displays works from the 19th and early 20th centuries by European artists who were inspired by their travels in North Africa and the Middle East. It provides a unique perspective on how Morocco was viewed through a Western lens.

* **Boucharouite Museum (Musée Boucharouite)**
    A vibrant and colorful museum dedicated to the "Boucharouite," which are traditional Berber carpets made from scraps of fabric. Housed in a traditional riad, it celebrates the creativity and resourcefulness of Berber women through its eclectic collection of folk art.

* ** The Aman museum , also called Marrakech Water Museum or Mohammed VI Museum for Water Civilization, is a museum in Marrakech devoted to water and hydraulic techniques in traditional Morocco. It is located at the northern entrance to Marrakech, in the Palmeraie sector.
