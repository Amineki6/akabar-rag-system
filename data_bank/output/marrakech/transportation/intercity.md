### Intercity Buses

#### Gare Routière de Marrakech (Bab Doukkala)
* **Location:** Located just outside the city walls at the Bab Doukkala gate.
* **Description:** This is the main bus station in Marrakech and serves as the central hub for regional and long-distance bus services. Companies like CTM and Supratours operate from here, offering routes to major destinations across Morocco, including Casablanca, Fes, Agadir, and Tangier.
* **Note:** It is recommended to book tickets in advance for popular routes, especially during peak travel times.


### Intercity Trains

#### Gare de Marrakech (Marrakech Train Station)
* **Location:** Avenue Hassan II, in the modern neighborhood of Gueliz.
* **Description:** The city's primary railway station, operated by the national railway company, ONCF. The station is modern and serves as a key gateway for travelers arriving from or departing to other major Moroccan cities. Petit taxis are readily available outside for connections to the Medina and other areas.
* **Key Destinations:** The train network connects Marrakech with Casablanca, Rabat, Fes, and Tangier (with a connection to the Al-Boraq high-speed train in Casablanca).
* **Note:** You can purchase tickets at the station or online through the official ONCF website. It is wise to book in advance, especially for overnight trains or during holidays. The ALSA bus L19 also stops here, providing a direct link to the airport and Jamaa El Fna.
