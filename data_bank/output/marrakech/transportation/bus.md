### Local Public Buses (ALSA)

The official ALSA website is the best source for the most current schedules and route maps: `https://www.alsa.ma/en/marrakech`

#### General Information
ALSA operates a comprehensive and very affordable public bus network in Marrakech, connecting the city center with the suburbs, airport, and nearby towns like Ourika and Tahanaout.

#### Practical Info
* **Fares:** A standard single journey ticket within the city typically costs around 4 MAD.
* **Cards:** ALSA offers rechargeable Ikhlas cards for frequent users.
* **How to Ride:** Signal the bus to stop. Board and purchase your ticket directly from the driver. Press the stop button before your intended destination to signal you wish to get off.

#### Key Bus Hubs
* **Arset El Bilk / Jamaa El Fna:** A central hub near the main square, serving as a starting point for numerous city lines.
* **Bab Doukkala:** The location of the main intercity bus station (`Gare Routière`) and a major hub for local buses.
* **Bab Ghmat:** Another key terminal, especially for routes heading out of the city.

#### Key Tourist Routes
* **L19 (Airport Bus):** This is the dedicated airport shuttle. It connects the airport directly with key points like **Jamaa El Fna** and the main train station. A one-way ticket costs 30 MAD.
* **L1:** Connects the central **Jamaa El Fna** area with **Bab Doukkala**.
* **L66:** Runs from **Jamaa El Fna** to the Socoma area.
* **L25:** Departs from the **Sidi Mimoun** area (near the Medina) for the popular destination of **Ourika**.
