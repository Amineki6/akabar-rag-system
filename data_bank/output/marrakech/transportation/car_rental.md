### Renting a Car

Renting a car in Marrakech offers the flexibility to explore the city and its surrounding regions, such as the Atlas Mountains or coastal Essaouira, at your own convenience.

#### Booking Process
It is advisable to book your car online in advance, particularly during peak tourist seasons, to ensure availability and secure more competitive rates. Rental agencies are located at **Marrakesh Menara Airport (RAK)** and throughout the city. Both major international and local rental companies are available.

* **Requirements:**
    * A valid driving license from your country of residence.
    * An International Driving Permit (IDP) may be required by some rental agencies. It is crucial to confirm this requirement before booking.

#### Expected Costs
Costs can vary significantly based on the type of car, rental duration, and the season. Manual transmission cars are generally more affordable than automatic ones.

* **Economy (e.g., Kia Picanto):** €15 - €30 per day.
* **Compact SUV:** €35 - €60 per day.
* **Luxury:** €80+ per day.

#### Insurance Guide
Most rental agreements include basic third-party liability insurance, but this often comes with a high excess (deductible). You should carefully review the coverage details.

* **Common Options:**
    * **Collision Damage Waiver (CDW):** This will reduce or eliminate your financial liability for damage to the rental vehicle in an accident.
    * **Personal Accident Insurance (PAI):** This covers medical costs for the driver and passengers. Check if your personal travel insurance already includes this before purchasing.
* **Pro Tip:** Many credit cards offer rental car insurance as a perk. Verify with your card provider if you are covered to avoid purchasing redundant insurance.

#### Driving in Marrakech
While driving in the old Medina is challenging and not recommended due to extremely narrow streets, navigating the new city (Gueliz) and the roads outside Marrakech is generally straightforward.

* Drive on the **right side of the road**.
* On roundabouts, yield to traffic already inside the circle.
* Be aware of speed cameras and strictly adhere to posted speed limits.
* Exercise caution for pedestrians, scooters, and donkey carts, especially near the Medina.
