### Overview of Marrakech's History

The provided documents trace the history of Marrakech from its founding in 1070  by Abu Bakr ibn Umar of the Almoravid dynasty, under whom it became an imperial capital. The city entered a golden age in 1147 after being conquered by the Almohads, who built some of its most iconic landmarks, including the Koutoubia Mosque and the Menara Gardens. Following a period of decline, Marrakech's status was revived in the 16th century when it became the capital of the Saadian dynasty, who built magnificent structures like the El Badi Palace and the Saadian Tombs. The city was later neglected under the early Alaouite dynasty, which focused on its new capital in Meknes.

During the French Protectorate (1912-1956), the modern "new city" of Gueliz was built outside the historic ramparts, and the powerful Glaoui family governed the region in collaboration with the French. Since Morocco's independence in 1956, Marrakech has regained its status as a premier city and is now a world-renowned tourist destination. Its ancient medina was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1985. A significant recent event was the 6.9 magnitude earthquake on September 8, 2023, which was the strongest instrumentally recorded in Morocco and caused widespread damage, including the destruction of historic landmarks in Marrakech.
